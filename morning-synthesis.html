Iran Peace Text Confirmed, Strait of Hormuz Set to Reopen

Pakistan confirmed final agreed deal text at 21:30 HKT; with crude falling and the Nikkei up 3 percent, Asia treasury desks must judge whether political text becomes operational ceasefire before Sunday's open.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Pakistan confirmed at 21:30 HKT that 'final, agreed upon text' of a US-Iran peace deal exists. Washington has not denied it. The Strait of Hormuz, the narrow Persian Gulf chokepoint through which roughly 20 percent of traded oil transits, is set to reopen. Oil fell sharply; the Nikkei opened up more than 3 percent. US forces shot down two Iranian drones near Hormuz even as Trump declared the deal done, a gap between political text and operational ceasefire that should keep Asia credit desks with commodity hedges cautious until a joint statement lands.

SpaceX closed its market debut up 19 percent after raising $75 billion, making Musk the first person to hold a trillion-dollar net worth. For Hong Kong and Singapore asset managers carrying US tech exposure, private-market multiples now have a live public benchmark to trade against.

The UK contracted in April, the first hard reading showing the Iran conflict transmitting into European output. China sanctioned the Philippine defence secretary over 'erroneous remarks', a signal Southeast Asian compliance heads should track ahead of scheduled ASEAN exercises. US Section 702, the statute that permits American intelligence agencies to collect foreign nationals' communications, expired for the first time since 2008; bank compliance officers with US operations should log the lapse.
