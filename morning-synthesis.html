Fed Begins Warsh Era With Hawkish Lean as Iran Deal Approaches Signing

Kevin Warsh's first Fed meeting tilted hawkish with nearly half of officials seeing a 2026 hike; Iran's Hormuz reopening faces skepticism; and a US ruling just strengthened prosecutors' case against Huawei.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Three desks moved overnight, and Hong Kong reads each differently from the Atlantic consensus.

Kevin Warsh held rates at his first Federal Reserve meeting but nearly half of officials penciled in a 2026 hike. The Hong Kong dollar peg means HIBOR, the interbank lending rate, tracks any Fed move within weeks. Private bankers and credit officers should have this before the session opens.

Iran dominates the Atlantic feed. The US-Iran memorandum moves toward signing, but Bloomberg's energy sources flag real skepticism about how fast the Strait of Hormuz, through which a third of global LNG transits, actually reopens. Asian importers are ahead of that timeline.

The story SCMP ran and FT did not: a US federal judge ruled Meng Wanzhou's admissions from the Canadian extradition proceeding admissible at Huawei's criminal trial. Any compliance officer managing Huawei-linked exposure needs legal review today.

SpaceX closed its $60 billion acquisition of Cursor, the AI coding platform, as Trump officials separately sought a truce with Anthropic. Both moves accelerate AI supply chain consolidation that will shape every G7 AI-access negotiation this year.

Watch today: Iran signing confirmation and any Warsh statement on the 2026 rate path.
