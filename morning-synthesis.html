Chip Shortage Extends to 2027 as Anthropic Names Alibaba in AI Theft Claim

Micron's blowout forecast locks in the AI infrastructure trade through 2027, while Anthropic's accusation against Alibaba redraws the boundary between competition and extraction for every APAC technology buyer.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Micron's earnings beat is the overnight number that matters. The company projects the memory chip shortage running past 2027, Bloomberg reports, and global equities rallied on the read-through for the AI buildout cycle. For a Singapore private banker or Hong Kong asset manager positioned in semiconductor names, this is a supply-constraint confirmation with a named duration. A two-year shortage horizon changes capex plans at every hyperscaler and changes the procurement calculus for banks building private AI infrastructure. Anthropic has accused Alibaba of illicitly extracting capabilities from the Claude model, Reuters reports. The allegation, if substantiated, would be the clearest named instance of model-weight appropriation by a Chinese technology company and lands directly on the US congressional hearing this week that warned Chinese economic espionage has expanded to AI, a story already on our homepage. A CISO at a bank using Claude via API should note that the exposure vector named here is on the provider side, not the enterprise integration layer. OpenAI's first custom chip, built with Broadcom and unveiled this week, tightens the vertical stack further, shortening the distance between model training and silicon dependency. Germany's Deutsche Bahn rail network went dark overnight after an IT maintenance fault cascaded into a nationwide outage, The Guardian reports; the sequence is a clean case study in single-point infrastructure failure at scale. The Iran war powers friction between Trump and Senate Republicans has not resolved, with a Senate vote rejecting the resolution after a closed-door clash that NBC and BBC both covered in detail. Watch the PCE inflation print today, which arrives into a market already elevated on the Micron move.
