Shun Hing Breach Adds HK Names to a SharePoint Fire Drill Already Underway

A one million-record breach at a Hong Kong conglomerate lands the same week CISA confirms active exploitation of the SharePoint flaw HK enterprises have not patched.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

The overnight lead for this desk is local. Shun Hing Group, the Hong Kong conglomerate behind Panasonic and Toshiba distribution locally, disclosed a breach exposing data on roughly one million people, arriving days after CISA confirmed active exploitation of a Microsoft SharePoint remote code execution flaw, the document-collaboration platform sitting behind most HK enterprise intranets. A compliance officer at any HK firm running on-premises SharePoint checks patch status this morning, not next week. Cheung Kwok-keung's column on Wang Chi House carries the day's sharper read: the undamaged Wang Fuk Court tower left the government buyback scheme faster than the burnt ones, which says the deal was never really about fire damage, it was about exit liquidity dressed as compensation. Elsewhere, Google lost its final EU appeal and owes 4.1 billion euro over Android antitrust, a number European regulators will cite the next time they size a case against a US platform. Russia hit Kyiv with its largest barrage of the war, killing at least 27. US June payrolls came in at 57,000, missing forecasts, and a Singapore based portfolio manager rereads the Fed's September odds before the open. Watch whether HKMA or any APAC regulator issues a SharePoint advisory before Monday.
