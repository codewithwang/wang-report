US-Iran Peace Deal Reaches Agreed Text as Nikkei Gains 3 Percent

Pakistan says the US-Iran peace text is agreed; Tehran has not confirmed. Nikkei is up 3 percent, oil is lower, and regional risk desks hold open positions against an unverified ceasefire.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Iran and the United States have reached what Pakistan calls 'final, agreed upon text' of a peace deal. The Nikkei gained more than 3 percent overnight, oil retreated. The Strait of Hormuz, the waterway carrying roughly a fifth of global seaborne oil, stands to reopen if the terms hold. Tehran has not confirmed what Islamabad described. The World Bank warned this week that a sustained conflict would push global growth to post-COVID lows; that scenario is conditional on text Tehran has not verified.

Charmaine Lo's weekend read makes the sequencing plain: markets moved on the Iran ceasefire before the text existed, and any risk desk still holding open positions faces a cover-or-hold decision this morning.

SpaceX closed its first trading day up 19 percent at a $2.2 trillion valuation, making Musk the world's first trillionaire. FT and Memeorandum led with that milestone; for APAC asset managers, the signal is what capital concentration at that scale does to venture and PE appetite across regional tech in the quarters that follow.

Beijing sanctioned the Philippine defence secretary over 'erroneous remarks,' a step that lands during World Cup week when ASEAN political bandwidth is thin. Yoon Suk Yeol received a prison sentence for the drone flights over Pyongyang, closing South Korea's most severe constitutional crisis since democratisation.
