US Blocks Foreign Nationals from Frontier AI as Iran Deal Text Is Agreed

Washington's order to Anthropic to cut foreign-national access to its flagship models lands over a weekend, leaving APAC enterprise AI teams without guidance until business resumes Monday.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Washington ordered Anthropic, the American AI lab behind Claude, to block foreign-national access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 on national security grounds. Any APAC bank running Claude-powered compliance workflows or regulator piloting frontier-model review needs clarity on whether its API access falls under the restriction before the Monday open. Pakistan confirmed US-Iran talks produced agreed deal text overnight; if signed, it reopens the Strait of Hormuz, the waterway carrying roughly 20 percent of global seaborne oil, and any private banker with commodity or shipping exposure should watch for the reopening announcement. US forces struck Iranian drones near the strait while the text was being confirmed, a reminder that agreed language and operational stand-down are not the same moment. Beijing's top Taiwan affairs official offered a 'joint answer' formulation on cross-strait peace, language Taipei trading desks parse in real time while Washington registers it as background. China arrested an American scholar-journalist, one more friction point in a bilateral already stretched by tungsten export curbs pushing Japanese chipmakers toward production halts. SpaceX debuted on Nasdaq above $2 trillion, closing in on Amazon by market cap. Charmaine Lo's weekend read frames the cycle: Hong Kong's SFC, the securities regulator, rewrote fund distribution rules, Washington rewrote its landmarks, and the gap between what frameworks cover and what they fix held.
