Hormuz Closed, Switzerland Talks Begin; Lebanon May Unravel Both

Iran shut the Strait of Hormuz overnight and the first US-Iran diplomatic round opens in Switzerland Sunday, but Lebanon's border arrangement, unaddressed in either framework, is the variable that determines both outcomes.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

The Strait of Hormuz, the waterway carrying roughly 20 percent of global oil supply, closed overnight as Iran cited Israeli strikes on Hezbollah positions in Lebanon as ceasefire violations; no vessel seizures had been confirmed at 06:00 HKT. US-Iran talks open their first round in Switzerland Sunday, short the signing ceremony nixed last week. NYT identifies Lebanon's border arrangement as the main obstacle, distinct from the nuclear sequencing the Switzerland talks were designed to address. Senate Republicans broke from Trump on the deal Friday; JD Vance told Israeli critics Trump is their only remaining ally.

For a Hong Kong commodity desk or Singapore tanker operator, LNG and crude futures open Sunday under the Hormuz announcement without confirmed enforcement. Japan's G7 rare earth proposal, flagged by SCMP as risking further regional tension, adds a second supply-chain pressure.

Aya Nakamura's column today maps the same decoupling through AI compute: the BIS, Commerce's export enforcement arm, suspended Fable 5 while Huawei's 910C kept shipping. For a CIO reviewing AI vendor contracts in APAC, compute diversification is now a risk management question with a demonstrated enforcement precedent.

Hong Kong: the PLA opened barracks for the 29th handover anniversary; welfare authorities pledged an age-threshold review after another elderly resident died without a welfare contact record, three days after a 224-page poverty framework was released.
