Hormuz Reopens Friday as Washington Suspends Fable 5 and Beijing Blocks Infineon

The overnight cycle moved oil prices, suspended Washington's AI cybersecurity models, and shut Infineon, the German chipmaker, out of China's gallium nitride market; that third story will run longest for APAC supply chains.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

The US-Iran framework deal reopens the Strait of Hormuz, the narrow chokepoint through which roughly a fifth of global seaborne oil transits, by Friday. Crude fell hard; equities rallied. Asset managers with Gulf exposure or regional inflation hedges in APAC portfolios need to reassess their positions before the open. SpaceX's $85.7bn IPO close, the largest in US history, landed in the same cycle, adding a second data point for capital allocation desks.

Washington invoked national security authority to order Anthropic to block global access to its cybersecurity AI models, Fable 5 included. Today's column by Aya Nakamura documents the selective logic: the identical jailbreak cited in the suspension order also appears in GPT-5.5, which faces no equivalent action. Any APAC bank or fintech with compliance or threat-intelligence workflows on Anthropic's stack needs a vendor continuity review today. HK and Singapore bank CISOs are the first call.

The story Western desks did not surface: a Chinese court blocked Infineon, the German power semiconductor company, from selling gallium nitride chips in mainland China. GaN underlies 5G base stations and EV charging infrastructure across the region. Domestic producers surged. This is the supply chain story of the week. G7 opens in France this morning. Watch whether allies ratify the Iran terms or fracture on the Israeli exception.
