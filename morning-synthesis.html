Treasury Yields at 2007 High Drag Asia Into a Fourth-Day Slide

The 30-year has not been this elevated since the GFC run-up; for APAC duration books and real-money allocators, the move is fast enough to force a decision today.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

The 30-year Treasury printed above its 2007 high overnight, pulling Asian equities into a fourth straight decline; the bond market is pricing an inflation scenario that central bank forward guidance has not yet confirmed, and that gap between the rate curve and official commentary is where the decision lives for real-money allocators on the MTR this morning. Putin lands in Beijing today for Xi talks, with a long-stalled natural gas project the centrepiece, and the commodity pricing implications of any agreement would feed back into Asian inflation expectations within the quarter.

On the cyber desk, today's column by Kai Tanner runs alongside Krebs's overnight confirmation that a CISA administrator left AWS GovCloud credentials in a public GitHub repository. Two credential-in-version-control incidents in one week, one of them originating from the agency that sets US federal remediation timelines: the compliance gap is not abstract for any organisation running workloads under FedRAMP or US regulatory audit.

Google's Gemini 3.5 Flash released overnight, and a federal jury ruled for OpenAI against Musk, both developments accelerating the foundation model race at the same moment Aya Nakamura's column argues no US enforcement framework covers ChatGPT's banking integration. The liability surface is expanding faster than the regulatory map.
