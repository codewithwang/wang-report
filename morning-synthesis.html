Chip Rally Turns Historic as Lebanon Advances and Iran Framework Deteriorates

AI bubble math, an Israeli ground advance to Beaufort Castle, and Trump's hardened Iran terms arrive in the same overnight cycle and compress the APAC risk manager's Monday morning agenda.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Bloomberg's analysis finds the current chip-stock run historically anomalous, forcing the AI bubble debate from theoretical to immediate: for the asset manager in Hong Kong holding Nvidia-adjacent positions, this week tests whether the rally is positioning crowding or a genuine repricing. Israel's seizure of Beaufort Castle, the hilltop fortress controlling the southern Lebanese road network, is the deepest ground advance in 25 years and closes the most plausible de-escalation corridor. The aluminum supply chain is absorbing simultaneous shocks from Gulf disruption and US tariff schedules, a dual-input structure that single-variable commodity models will understate; the bank treasurer hedging aluminum-adjacent industrial exposure needs two numbers, not one. Congo's decision to triple royalty rates for lithium miners lands inside a battery supply chain every APAC electrification roadmap is already treating as the binding constraint. Charmaine Lo's weekend column has more weight this morning: Hong Kong's emergency alert window at fifteen minutes, set during Beijing's 100-ship naval encirclement exercise last week, is not a planning curiosity for the bank CISO whose continuity protocols assumed 72-hour warning cycles. Trump's toughened Iran nuclear terms, transmitted after Friday's close, hardened an already fractured negotiation. Watch Tehran's official response before the New York open.
