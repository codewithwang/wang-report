Iran Breaks April Ceasefire as ECB Prepares First G7 War-Inflation Hike

For APAC banks and asset managers, the Iran-Israel exchange is now two problems at once: a fuel shock threatening airline credit and a rates event that will move every Asian yield curve this week.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Iran launched ballistic missiles at Israel overnight Saturday, the first direct strike since the April ceasefire, and the US struck Iranian sites before Sunday's first light. The ECB, the European Central Bank, is primed to raise rates this week as the first G7 institution moving on Iran war inflation. Airlines are warning of a $100 billion profit hit from the fuel surge; for a Hong Kong credit desk, that is a solvency question. South Korea moved to stop the won from extending its slide to the weakest level since 2009, pledging intervention against speculative positioning, a regional FX stress signal APAC treasury teams should read alongside the airline exposure. Beijing dispatched its largest patrol vessel east of Taiwan within hours of Japan and the Philippines concluding maritime boundary talks, a sequence SCMP tracked that outlets running Iran ledes missed. Meta confirmed thousands of Instagram accounts were taken through its own AI chatbot, the AI attack surface in consumer infrastructure arriving faster than security teams modeled. Magnus Honeyfield's column today on SFC e-distribution rules, the Securities and Futures Commission's new framework for digital product sales in Hong Kong, names the same gap in climate finance: the channel lowers costs but no insurer has products covering the risk it reaches. Watch the ECB decision and any Manila response to Beijing's patrol.
