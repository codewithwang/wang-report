Seoul Circuit Breaker Fires as Iran War Reaches Asian Markets at Open

Korean equities halted and Japanese stocks down 4% at the open: the Middle East strikes are now a balance-sheet event for every APAC portfolio manager.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

South Korea's KOSPI, the benchmark equity index in Seoul, triggered a market-wide circuit breaker Monday after falling more than 8%, and Japan's Nikkei 225 dropped 4% at the open. Indonesia's sovereign bonds, rupiah, and equities are selling off in parallel. The Iran-Israel exchange of airstrikes is the proximate cause; oil prices are rising, and Asian markets are absorbing the first full settlement session before London wakes. An asset manager with Korean tech exposure or a compliance head holding emerging-market bonds has live decisions this morning.

Xi Jinping arrived in Pyongyang on Monday, the first Chinese head of state to visit North Korea since 2019. SCMP reports he is entering a country that has materially upgraded its conventional and nuclear capacity on revenue from the Russia-Ukraine war. The summit opens while Asian markets fall; FT's Iran lede could not hold both.

Magnus Honeyfield's column today on the SFC's e-distribution rules, Hong Kong's framework for digitally selling financial products, makes the point that instruments designed to close APAC's climate protection gap remain outside it. Lower distribution costs, unresolved structural gap.

The IMF managing director warned last week the world is not ready for compounding shocks. Monday's open is the data point.
