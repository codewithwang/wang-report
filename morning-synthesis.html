Nasdaq Chip and Memory Rout Leaves APAC Equity Books on Alert for Monday

A 4 percent Nasdaq selloff driven by chip and memory stocks arrived Friday; every APAC portfolio with Taiwan or Korean semiconductor exposure now faces Monday's open.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Nasdaq shed 4 percent Friday in its sharpest single-session drop since early 2025, chip and memory stocks leading: TSMC's US-listed depositary receipts and SK Hynix both closed deep in the red. Asset managers with APAC tech exposure need to decide before Monday whether this is a flush or the start of a correction. The US May payrolls print, 172,000 jobs against an 88,000 consensus, reinforced the pressure, lifting bets toward a Federal Reserve rate rise by year-end. Japan's real wages rose 1.9 percent in April, a fourth consecutive gain, keeping the Bank of Japan on its normalization track and pressuring the yen carry trade, where cheap yen funds higher-yielding positions. Geopolitically, Xi Jinping arrives in Pyongyang with reduced leverage: Kim's Russia alliance has narrowed Beijing's pull. Beijing separately banned four New Zealand lawmakers from entering China after their Taiwan delegation last month, a precise signal on where cross-strait contact sits. Anthropic, freshly valued at $965 billion, called for a global AI pause; bank compliance heads running AI-vendor roadmaps should flag whether this posture creates new contractual uncertainty. Back in Hong Kong, the Observatory's extreme-heat warning has the HK$17 public pool at queue-before-eight capacity, the scene Cheung Kwok-keung's weekend column describes.
