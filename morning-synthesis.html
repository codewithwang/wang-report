Trump Claims Iran Deal, Hormuz Still Closed, Inflation at 4.2%

The Strait of Hormuz remains blocked despite Trump's deal talk, and US inflation running at 4.2 percent tells the region what is coming on rates.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Iran formally closed the Strait of Hormuz, the chokepoint carrying roughly a fifth of global oil by volume, even as Trump announced Thursday night he was calling off the latest strikes and claimed negotiations had reached senior Iranian leadership. The strait stays closed until a verifiable agreement is signed. FT and Google News led with the deal claim; neither ran the closure.

US inflation for May came in at 4.2 percent, the highest since February 2023. The European Central Bank, the Frankfurt institution that sets rates for the 20-country euro area, is now expected to raise rates for the first time since September 2023, driven directly by energy disruption. A private banker with European bond exposure needs to reassess interest-rate sensitivity today.

Japan and Malaysia announced bilateral energy security cooperation Thursday, a direct hedge against Hormuz dependency that Western outlets did not feature. Tehran has since hit Bahraini, Kuwaiti, and Jordanian infrastructure, widening the conflict's geographic spread. Gold is swinging on each strike-and-pause cycle. Cybersecurity researchers are publicly contesting the content restrictions on Anthropic's Fable, the company's newest frontier model, with direct implications for AI-assisted red-team tooling.

A bank security chief running AI-assisted red-team programs needs to assess those Fable guardrails before market open.
