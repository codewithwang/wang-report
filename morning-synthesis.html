Iran Deal Final Call Expected Today; PBOC Has Already Moved

Trump's Iran framework decision arrives this weekend as PBOC has already positioned on the ceasefire window, and for Asian banks and asset managers the gap between Beijing's move and Washington's indecision is live.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Iran dominates the overnight cycle. Trump will deliver a final determination on the nuclear framework this weekend; NYT reports both sides are closing on preliminary terms, with the Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly a fifth of the world's oil passes, the last serious sticking point.

Vincent Lai's PBOC column changes the picture: China's central bank has treated the 60-day ceasefire as an energy financing window, positioning before the monetary easing the tariff agreement requires. For an asset manager modelling oil-price scenarios, or a bank risk officer watching Chinese infrastructure investment flows, the gap between China's action and Washington's indecision is the live question.

Hegseth arrives at the Shangri-La Dialogue in Singapore this morning, Asia's annual defence gathering, with US allies watching for any change in US posture following last year's public tensions. Japan and the Philippines announced a comprehensive strategic partnership, a formal upgrade in bilateral ties, the latest block in the APAC security architecture being assembled around US commitment uncertainty. Asia's remittance corridors to Iran-adjacent economies remain under strain, an operational exposure compliance teams at HK and Singapore banks are managing directly.

AI governance: the UK Home Office will use AI models to estimate migrant ages from next year, a sovereign border deployment precedent APAC compliance officers should be tracking.
