From the desk · Daily take
DC
By Dev ChatterjeeSenior CorrespondentTue, Jun 2

Fenway Sports Group dismissed Arne Slot this week while the Premier League trophy was still in transit to Merseyside, which means FSG has held Liverpool FC for sixteen years, generated two Premier League titles and a Champions League, and decided that the man holding the most recent one is the variable that needs replacing. The banner is already in production. Slot's name is not on it. FSG's model since the £300 million acquisition in 2010 has been to hire managers with unconventional profiles at suppressed wages, run the squad below Manchester City and Arsenal's reported wage ceilings, and sell the surplus at peak value, a structure that functions until you win the league and the squad expects the economics to shift.

Richard Hughes, FSG's sporting director since April 2024, is running a managerial appointment inside a firm external deadline. UEFA schedules the Champions League group-stage draw for Nyon before the end of June, and any manager announced after that date inherits a fixture list he played no part in building. The shortlist will be short. FSG's historical preference is managers whose market value is depressed by club size or league prestige, which is why the early speculation in the Liverpool press runs toward the Bundesliga and Eredivisie rather than toward coaches whose previous employers would demand compensation to release them. Hughes runs the operation under an FSG mandate to build over a three-year window; Slot was year two, and the person who fills the vacancy before the Champions League draw in Nyon is year three's opening decision.
