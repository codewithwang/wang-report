From the desk · Daily take
By Dev ChatterjeeSenior CorrespondentSat, Jun 27

The Milwaukee Bucks traded Giannis Antetokounmpo to the Houston Rockets for a package reported at $285 million in contract value plus five first-round picks, which means an NBA franchise completed the most expensive organizational surrender in the league's salary-cap era while simultaneously finishing fourth in the Central Division with a .500 record the season it happened. The trade deadline was February; the front office had eight months before that to read the room, and the room was reading back.

What Houston gets is a 31-year-old two-time MVP on a supermax whose knees have been a recurring item in training-room notes since 2023, a fact the Rockets' medical staff signed off on and the Bucks' front office priced in as Houston's problem. The Bucks keep the picks, the cap flexibility, and the question of whether Damian Lillard at 35 constitutes a rebuild or a farewell tour -- which is not a comfortable question to hold. Milwaukee's ownership group, led by Marc Lasry and Wes Edens, now has the asset sheet of a team in transition and the ticket-sales posture of a city that watched its franchise player leave twice in the same window. The draft-night press conference is scheduled for Tuesday, June 30, in Houston.

The Lillard line lands. Desk should confirm the June 30 presser date before it goes to layout. — WR
