Herbert Ties Major Record With Stunning 62 (LIV Golf)

Todd Herbert shot a 62 at a LIV event this week, matching the lowest score ever posted in a major men's professional tour round, and the number is the whole story: 62 shots to walk a course architects design specifically to punish 62s. That kind of round doesn't happen because a player got hot. It happens because a swing has stopped negotiating with the golf course and started dictating terms to it.

Herbert's 62 will get filed next to the handful of other rounds tied for the record, which is where PIF's four-billion-dollar golf experiment always said it wanted to live, next to history rather than apart from it. LIV still doesn't have the world ranking points or the field depth of a major, and one low round doesn't fix that math. But a record score puts Herbert's name in the conversation whether the ranking committees invite him or not. Records don't ask permission. Neither, lately, does the tour that produced one.

The 62 needs the field it was shot into named, wind, pin positions, whether contenders had already fallen away, or the number floats free of the round that produced it. Once a major ranking body responds to LIV scores on the record, run that decision against this piece's bet that records don't need permission. -- WR
