From the desk · Daily take
DC
By Dev ChatterjeeSenior CorrespondentFri, Jun 5

Klutch Sports is steering its star prospect toward Dallas, which means the most consequential roster decision the Mavericks will make this offseason is being made by an agency. Miriam Adelson paid north of three billion dollars for the franchise in 2023 without apparently acquiring the part that decides who plays on it. The Mavericks shipped Luka Doncic to the Lakers seventeen months ago and have spent the interval managing the gap between "we have a plan" and "this is the plan," the coaching search now pivoting again in parallel. Rich Paul's Klutch roster runs two dozen NBA players deep. Dallas is his best available rebuild canvas.

I caught this on a phone screen at the squash club in Wan Chai, my coach talking about someone else's positioning entirely, and what struck me about the Mavericks coverage was that the coaching pivot and the Klutch steering broke as two separate stories in the same news cycle. They are not separate. Both are Klutch stories. The NBA Draft is June 26, and the head coach Nico Harrison hires will walk into a locker room Rich Paul assembled before that coach ran a single practice.

The squash club paragraph does real work, not texture work. — WR
