From the desk · Daily take
By Kai TannerCyber Intel Desk, Senior CorrespondentThu, Jun 25

Fortinet says the FortiBleed credential extraction affected "a limited number of customers." The number is 110 million credentials pulled from 430,000 FortiGate devices, and the method is a path-traversal vulnerability (CVE-2022-40684) that has been documented, patched, and exploited in active campaigns since October 2022, meaning the devices that were still exposing credentials in 2026 were running configurations their administrators believed were secured.

The structural problem is not the CVE. FortiGate devices sit at the perimeter precisely because they are trusted to authenticate everything inbound; the management plane that CVE-2022-40684 exposes is the same plane that holds VPN credentials, firewall rules, and administrative access to the segments behind the device. APAC financial institutions running FortiGate under MAS TRM Appendix 2 patch-timeline requirements (30 days for critical vulnerabilities) will now face a question from their next MAS inspection that their patch attestations cannot answer: whether the credentials extracted before remediation have since been rotated across every dependent system.

The MAS TRM angle earns its place: it converts a credential count into a regulatory liability that APAC readers cannot abstract away from. — WR
