ABOUT THE WANG REPORT

The Wang Report is an independent news aggregation and editorial site, curated from Hong Kong. It surfaces the stories that matter — from geopolitics and cybersecurity to local Hong Kong news, sports, and health — and presents them in a format designed for fast, informed reading.

EDITORIAL APPROACH

Stories are sourced from major wire services (AP, Reuters, Bloomberg), regional outlets (SCMP, RTHK, Dimsum Daily), and specialised feeds. An AI-powered editorial engine ranks, deduplicates, and editorialises headlines in the Drudge Report tradition: bold, direct, and occasionally wry.

The "Today's Take" editorial digest on the main page features original commentary generated with AI assistance, offering a Hong Kong insider perspective on the day's top stories. Editorials aim to inform, not to criticise specific institutions or entities.

ORIGINAL CONTENT

While The Wang Report aggregates news from public sources, it adds original value through:

  • AI-powered editorial ranking and headline editorialisation
  • Original editorial commentary ("Today's Take" / Editor's Corner)
  • Long-form weekly columns by named correspondents (see Masthead)
  • Curated company watch tracking with editorial summaries
  • HK Kids Calendar, a curated guide to family events in Hong Kong

REFRESH SCHEDULE

The main page refreshes every 3 hours. Section pages (Geopolitical, Cyber, HK Local, etc.) refresh every 6 hours. Long-form columns publish on a weekly cadence per desk.

ABOUT THE CREATOR

The Wang Report is a personal project built and maintained from Hong Kong. It reflects one reader's habits, professional interests, and belief that news consumption should be fast, opinionated, and free of clutter.
