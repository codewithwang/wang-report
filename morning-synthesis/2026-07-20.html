Ninth Night of Strikes, Seventeenth American Death Confirmed

The Pentagon withheld troop injury figures from public tallies for weeks while the confirmed death toll reached 17, and Hong Kong's stablecoin queue and DigiCert's contested attribution both turn on the same reporting discipline the Pentagon just failed.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Nine straight nights of US strikes on Iran, and the story overnight is the arithmetic, not the ordnance. The New York Times reports the Pentagon withheld dozens of troop injuries from public tallies even as the confirmed death toll reached 17, a gap that should worry anyone who has been citing official casualty counts as complete. The same problem runs through today's top column: Kai Tanner's read on the DigiCert certificate breach shows Expel's attribution to a Chinese state linked group rested on a malware signature, not DigiCert's own forensics, and this week's wire coverage upgraded 'linked to' into 'confirmed.' A CISO briefing a board on that breach this week should cite Expel's actual hedge, not the flattened version. Brent crossed 90 dollars a barrel as strikes reached a Kuwaiti oil facility and the Caspian Pipeline Consortium halted loadings after a drone strike, so the widening is now hitting supply directly, not just sentiment. Kimi K3 matched Claude's coding benchmarks at a third of the price the same week Nvidia halved its Asia buyer list, a pairing Aya Nakamura's column argues does not cancel out. Hong Kong's red rainstorm warning is a Tuesday morning nuisance next to that. Watch Friday's war risk pricing in Brent futures.
