Israel Seizes Beaufort Castle in Deepest Lebanon Advance Since 2000

Three threads converge Monday: an Israeli ground push tightening the Iran resupply corridor, nuclear talks that may be structurally broken, and a chip-stock rally Wall Street is prepared to hold.
Walter Wang · WR Editor-in-Chief · Hong Kong

Israeli forces captured Beaufort Castle, a Crusader hilltop fortress commanding the Litani River valley, in Lebanon's deepest Israeli ground advance in twenty-five years. The evacuation order now runs to the Zahrani River. The corridor the advance seals is how Hezbollah, Iran's Lebanese political-military arm, receives material from Syria.

Trump simultaneously sent Tehran harder terms on the nuclear framework. Iran's lead negotiator says distrust of Washington is structural. The positions are now apart on premise, not detail.

Congo reclassified lithium as a strategic mineral this week, tripling the government royalties mines must pay. Combined with the aluminum shock from Gulf shipping disruption and accumulated US tariffs, upstream materials costs are rising from two directions for hardware and battery manufacturers at once. Bloomberg calls the chip-stock rally historic; the AI bubble debate has moved from analytical sidebar to live fund-manager question. An APAC asset manager or private banker with semiconductor exposure needs a position before end of week, not next.

Charmaine Lo's weekend piece belongs here: Hong Kong's emergency alert system gave fifteen minutes of warning the week Beijing ran a large naval encirclement exercise near Taiwan, and the post office required a multi-billion bailout in the same window. Fifteen minutes is not a planning horizon.
