By Rachel LamFinance & Risk Desk, Senior CorrespondentMon, Jun 1

Charles Li's IPO Link proposal, laid out at a forum this week, is a bet that the Southbound Stock Connect's ownership problem is fixable through registration rather than through price discovery (which is precisely what the proposal avoids addressing). The current Connect structure parks mainland buyers' economic interests through HKSCC as nominee, so shares count toward ownership but not toward HKEX registered free float for MSCI and FTSE Russell turnover calculations, the thresholds that quietly exclude mid-cap HKEX issuers whose cornerstone-heavy cap tables fail the turnover filter at each index review. Li wants to convert those economic positions into legal registered holdings at IPO. That changes the free-float math. It does not change a single cornerstone allocation.

So the arithmetic is this: if Connect buyers can subscribe directly at IPO and take registered positions, the turnover-threshold problem corrects at formation rather than at lock-up expiry, which is when current issuers discover their cornerstone-heavy cap table has already missed the index inclusion window. The question Li's successors at HKEX's Listings and Market Structure Division need to answer before any pilot goes live is whether index providers will recategorize Connect-originated registered positions as internationally accessible free float, given that those holders still cannot short, still cannot access derivatives, and still track PRC policy signals rather than global earnings cycles. MSCI's methodology review on exactly this point is scheduled for Q3 2026.

The desk should flag the Q3 MSCI review date before that window closes. — WR
