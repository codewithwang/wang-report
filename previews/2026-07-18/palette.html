Color scheme proposal -- Stamp & Ink -- 2026-07-18

The scheme already lives in the logo and the best pages: a bone paper ground, ink navy for words, and one red, the seal red of a Chinese chop, spent sparingly. This page codifies it so every page draws from the same five colors, and adds a quiet per-desk tone so you can feel which desk you are on without the site breaking into nine brands.

Core -- the whole site uses only these

Bone
#F9F8F6
Page ground. Never pure white.
Ink
#0B1F3A
Text, rules, nameplate.
Seal Red
#B0241E
The one accent. Section tags, hot stories, the chop.
Warm Rule
#C8C0B0
Hairlines, borders, dividers.
Panel
#F0EFED
Quiet boxes, pulled quotes.
Slate
#5A6470
Bylines, dates, captions. Floor for any gray text.

Rules that come with it: the site is light, no dark panels anywhere. Seal red is rationed, if everything is red nothing is. Grays never drop below slate for reading text. The dark navy score ribbons die in the desk-hub redesign.

Desk tones -- so each desk feels like its own room

Each desk keeps the core scheme and adds one muted accent, used only for its section tags, score strip, and link hovers, plus a barely-there wash on the page ground. Same paper, different ink in the stamp pad. HK Local keeps the seal red itself, the city is the home desk.
