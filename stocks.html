By Charmaine LoNews AnchorWed, Jul 8

Big 3 tapes closed flat today. No signal on the surface, plenty of dispersion underneath, and that's where this portfolio actually lives. Samsung's earnings triggered a chip sector selloff that dragged AMD down 8% and Intel down 10%, a reminder that semiconductor sentiment now trades as one correlated basket regardless of company fundamentals. Rate mood stayed quiet. Today's story was sector rotation, not policy.

Technology, the portfolio's most concentrated book, split hard along old-versus-new fault lines. CrowdStrike extended its run to a seventh straight winning day, up 18% over that stretch. Adobe drew a fresh Sell rating from BofA on AI disruption concerns. NVIDIA faces headlines about DeepSeek quietly building its own chip to route around US export controls. Consumer cyclical names diverged too: Amazon leaned into the AI buildout with a 25 billion dollar bond sale, while Lululemon, down 45% on the year, is left arguing its own turnaround case.

The advisors have flagged NVIDIA, the largest position, for trimming toward a smaller share of the book, staged across three tranches over four to six weeks, with proceeds seeding a new stake in Berkshire Hathaway. Beyond that, they're eyeing a healthcare allocation through UNH or JNJ and an international sleeve via VXUS, with hard exit rules on SoundHound and Unusual Machines. Intel, meanwhile, has been told to stop lingering. Cut it or commit.
