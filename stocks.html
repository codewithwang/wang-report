By Charmaine LoNews AnchorMon, Jul 13

Nasdaq futures slid on the Iran strike headlines this morning. Index prints closed flat, but the tape had the texture of a market deciding whether a geopolitical shock is a headline or a trend. Rates stayed quiet, which in this environment counts as good news. Chipmakers led the volatility, with Nvidia and Micron flagged as approaching buy points even as the broader tape wobbled, risk off and buy the dip sharing a news cycle without apparent contradiction.

Technology carried the portfolio's temperature today, and it ran a fever. Apple's new M6, M7 and M8 chip lineup drew attention as the clearest signal yet of AI reshaping its product architecture. Adobe took a downgrade from Bank of America that sat uneasily next to Intel's price hikes on server chips, AI demand straining supply even for the legacy giant. AMD cut prices on its mid-range chip to compete directly with Nvidia. Consumer names had a rougher day: Lululemon faces a Texas probe into toxic chemicals in its activewear, the kind of headline that outlasts the stock move that follows it.

Okta drew analyst attention as a buying opportunity after its recent pullback. Her advisors have been busiest with architecture rather than single names: trimming the concentrated technology book's largest position in Nvidia in staged tranches, using the proceeds to open a new position in Berkshire Hathaway, and laying groundwork for a healthcare allocation and international diversification in the weeks ahead. They have also proposed hard exit rules for the portfolio's more speculative names. The market gave them fresh reason today.
