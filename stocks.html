From the desk · Daily read
By Charmaine LoNews AnchorTue, Jul 7

Macro data was thin on the tape today. Rate anxiety and China tech policy did the talking in its place. Broadcom locking in Apple's chip business through 2031 read as a vote of confidence in the supply chain. A survey of Chinese firms walking away from Nvidia toward homegrown silicon read as the opposite, a reminder that the AI trade has a geopolitics problem no engineer can solve. Analysts spent the day warning that the S&P 500 ETF sitting in most portfolios is more concentrated, and more fragile, than its diversified reputation suggests. Diversified was the word on the label. Concentrated is the word in the filing.

Technology is carrying the weight and the risk in equal measure. It's still the largest position in the book, even as the names inside it pull against each other: Adobe nursing a maximum severity ColdFusion flaw under active exploitation, AMD catching an upgrade on earnings optimism from Goldman, Nvidia facing the China question directly. Consumer cyclical had its own rough morning. Amazon trimmed jobs in Washington State. Lululemon is fending off a lawsuit over tariff markups shoppers say never came back down. Software infrastructure was the bright spot, Okta earning a price target lift from Scotiabank.

Her advisors have opened discussions on trimming the Nvidia position in staged tranches over the coming weeks, redeploying the proceeds into a new position in Berkshire Hathaway. Further out, they're weighing a healthcare allocation through UnitedHealth or Johnson & Johnson, international diversification via Vanguard's ex-US fund, and firm exit rules for the portfolio's more speculative names, SoundHound AI and Unusual Machines, before the next double or the next thesis break. Intel, they say, needs a decision. Not another quarter of holding pattern.
