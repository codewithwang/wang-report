Macro data stayed quiet today, and the indices offered no headline in either direction. That left room for stock stories, and this book had no shortage of them.

The technology book, still the largest and most concentrated corner of this portfolio, moved on its own logic. NVIDIA drew attention less for its fundamentals than for a disclosure that a purchase preceded a tariff reversal, timing regulators tend to find interesting. Intel offered the better story, reporting a fix to its 18A yield problem and a doubling of wafer output, rare good manufacturing news for a name that has had little of it lately. Okta extended an eight day rally of 22%. Amazon's Kuiper network passed 400 satellites, a consumer cyclical name increasingly behaving like an infrastructure play. Apple sits under scrutiny in India over a data leak tied to a supplier. Lululemon made headlines for a theft, not an earnings print.

Her advisors have been busy. The near-term plan trims NVIDIA down to a smaller share of the book, in three tranches over four to six weeks, with proceeds seeding a new position in Berkshire Hathaway. Waiting in the wings: a healthcare allocation, likely UNH or JNJ, and international diversification through VXUS. The advisors have also proposed hard exit rules on the portfolio's smaller, speculative names, and flagged Intel for a binary decision. Commit or cut. No limbo.
