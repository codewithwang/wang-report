By Charmaine LoNews AnchorTue, Jul 14

Wall Street kept score against itself. Nvidia and Micron collected the AI-buildout trophy this week, while Amazon and the other mega caps got framed as yesterday's growth story. The broader tape wasn't reported at close, but the tone was rotation, not retreat, and risk appetite held up even as analysts argued over who deserves the credit. The Vanguard S&P 500 fund closed at 694, up nearly eleven percent on the year, carried largely by the chip names doing the heavy lifting underneath it.

Technology, still the largest sleeve in this book, had a split day. Nvidia took a Beijing-driven cut to its China buyer list. Apple picked up a rare bearish note from KeyBanc questioning demand. AMD investors were told to watch for news from Lisa Su in early August. Adobe was the exception, drawing fresh bullish calls after its forty percent slide from highs, the sort of note that arrives after the selling, not before it. Consumer cyclical had its own rough patch, with Lululemon hitting a six year low, though Barclays found something to like in the new chief executive. Software infrastructure carried its own headline risk: Okta flagged hackers vishing their way into Microsoft 365 accounts, proof the threat moved to the phone line while everyone watched the firewall.

Her advisors have been busy. The current guidance trims Nvidia down over several weeks and tranches, putting part of the proceeds into a new position in Berkshire Hathaway. Healthcare and international exposure are next to be built out, alongside firm exit rules being drafted for the portfolio's smaller speculative names. Intel remains a decision still owed, not a position to hold indefinitely., CL
