By Charmaine LoNews AnchorSat, Jul 4

Rate mood held steady today. The major indices traded in a narrow band, and the tape showed little conviction either way.

The real story sat underneath: semiconductors. Nvidia poached a Microsoft sales chief on a rich pay package, a hire that says plenty about how much fight is left in that rivalry. Apple's foldable phone bet got the credit for steadying the broader index against the chip sector's wobble.

The portfolio's technology book, still its most concentrated exposure, had its own busy day. AMD extended a run that has already made it one of the year's standout large caps. Adobe caught an upgrade that lifted the stock. Okta stretched an eight-session rally into a serious percentage gain, proof that software infrastructure has quietly outrun the chip names it usually rides alongside. Consumer cyclical had a stranger morning. Amazon advanced its satellite internet buildout toward launch. Lululemon made headlines for a theft, not an earnings print, evidence that a very good stock can still have a very bad news day.

Her advisors have been working through the concentration question directly. They are discussing a gradual trim of the largest position in staged tranches over the coming weeks, with proceeds seeding a new stake in Berkshire Hathaway. Also on the table: a first step into healthcare and international allocation, firm exit rules for the portfolio's more speculative names, and a decision, one way or the other, on Intel.
