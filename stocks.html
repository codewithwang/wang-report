By Charmaine LoNews AnchorWed, Jul 15

NVIDIA slides after Beijing tightened the buyer list, and the tape is reading it as a supply headline, not a demand one. That reading is doing more work than it should for the Direxion leveraged semiconductor sleeve, and geared bets on Nvidia are the first to feel it when the stock actually moves. Cybersecurity got a lift after IBM's Krishna flagged a shift in enterprise AI spending, which favors the portfolio's software infrastructure name. The broad index fund carries on regardless, absorbing all of it without comment.

Consumer names split. Amazon drew a lengthy financial comparison to SpaceX that its own balance sheet does not support, and Lululemon is now defending its valuation in court rather than on the sales floor. Apple's Siri rebuild reaching public beta gave the stock a story that has nothing to do with iPhone units for once, and Adobe's acquisition of Topaz Labs, alongside tripling AI revenue, gave that name one of its cleaner days this year.

Her advisors have been trimming the largest position, NVIDIA, in stages over the coming weeks, with proceeds earmarked to open a new Berkshire Hathaway stake, a hedge against the single-name concentration this portfolio has carried in technology. They are also planning a phased build into healthcare and international exposure, drafting exit rules for the more speculative names, and deciding, at last, whether Intel is a holding or a habit.
