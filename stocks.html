By Charmaine LoNews AnchorFri, Jul 10

Amazon rattled its investors, Lululemon fell with the rest of retail, Nvidia dropped on a training-methodology headline out of xAI, and Broadcom took the other side of that trade on an Apple chip deal. That is the tape in one sentence: a market still deciding whether the AI story is a monolith or a stock picker's market, and today's action voted for the latter.

The portfolio is feeling that split directly. The technology book, the largest and most concentrated corner of it, is pulling in opposite directions at once, Apple lifted by the Broadcom read-through while Nvidia and the broader chip complex absorb a fear that AI infrastructure spending gets rationed. Consumer names are having a rougher day, Lululemon down alongside its mall neighbors, and software infrastructure is not immune either, with Okta sliding harder than the market around it. The index and energy sleeves are the quiet part of the book this week. Diversification did its job.

On individual names, the advisors have been circling Nvidia specifically, discussing trimming it down from its outsized perch in stages over the coming weeks rather than all at once, with proceeds earmarked to start a new position in Berkshire Hathaway. They have also flagged Intel as a name that needs a decision rather than a shrug, and set informal guardrails around the portfolio's smaller, more speculative technology names. Healthcare and international exposure are on the drawing board. Neither has made it onto the tape.
