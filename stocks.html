By Charmaine LoNews AnchorTue, Jul 7

Macro benchmarks weren't printing clean levels this session, so call it a quiet tape by default rather than a calm one. Rate mood remains the backdrop it's been all year: chips get the headlines, but the cost of capital is still writing the script nobody credits it for. Semiconductors took a premarket bruising, with Intel sliding alongside Lam Research and Datadog, proof the chip trade still moves as a herd even when the stories are different stocks entirely.

The portfolio's concentrated technology book had a mixed morning. Nvidia is fielding a denial of its own delay, always a confidence-inspiring genre. Adobe is patching a ColdFusion flaw already under active attack. Apple got a vote of durability, locking in Broadcom chip supply through 2031, which in this industry counts as long-term planning. Consumer names had their own contrasts: Amazon drew fresh scrutiny over AI data center emissions, and Lululemon was sued over tariff price increases that, plaintiffs say, never came back down after the tariffs did. Okta had the cleanest morning of the lot, upgraded by Scotiabank with a lifted price target.

On individual names, the advisors have been doing quieter work than the headlines suggest. They're trimming Nvidia in stages over the coming weeks and using part of the proceeds to open a new Berkshire Hathaway position, a classic swap of momentum for ballast. They're also drafting firm rules for SoundHound and Unusual Machines: no further adds, trims on a double, exit if the thesis breaks. Intel is next up for judgment, commitment or a clean cut, with healthcare and international diversification on deck after that.
