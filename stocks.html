By Charmaine LoNews AnchorFri, Jul 17

Apple has retaken the crown as the world's most valuable company, edging past Nvidia in a swap that says less about either company's fundamentals than about how fast this market rotates its favorites. The broader tape today reflects that same restlessness, with megacap technology setting the pace and the rest of the market taking its cues.

Inside the technology sleeve, the story is less about winners than about repricing. Adobe sits 43 percent below its peak despite record revenue and tripling AI sales, which tells you the market is no longer paying for growth alone. It wants proof the growth is AI-native. Okta got the opposite treatment: an upgrade to overweight with a lifted price target, the reward for software infrastructure names that have had to earn confidence one client win at a time. Lululemon took a sell rating with more downside flagged. Consumer cyclical names are being judged on execution now, not brand.

On individual names, the advisors have been rebalancing rather than chasing headlines. They have discussed trimming Nvidia down to a smaller share of the technology book, in stages over the coming weeks, using the proceeds to open a new position in Berkshire Hathaway. They are also weighing new healthcare and international allocations, and drafting firmer rules around the portfolio's smaller speculative names. Intel remains a discussion. Not yet a decision., CL
