By Charmaine LoNews AnchorMon, Jul 6

Macro data stayed quiet on the tape today, with the major indices printing no fresh read. That silence was its own message: a market waiting to be told what to think.

The portfolio's sectors carried that split mood. Technology bore the weight and the worry in equal measure. Nvidia pushed its next-generation AI rack out to 2028, a delay that rippled through its suppliers and made one thing plain: the AI buildout runs on hardware schedules, not enthusiasm. Adobe drew a different kind of attention, a severe ColdFusion flaw now under active exploitation, already a problem for security teams before it becomes a headline anywhere else. Consumer cyclical had its own rough patch. Texas opened a probe into Lululemon over chemicals in its activewear, and the stock slid on the news. Software infrastructure supplied the day's bright spot: Okta picked up an upgraded price target from Scotiabank. Fixed income and the broader index exposure sat quietly in the background, doing what they are built to do.

Among individual names, Apple secured a multiyear chip agreement with Broadcom running through 2031, a long runway announced on a day when Amazon closed Mechanical Turk to new customers and AMD deepened its autonomous driving ambitions with a stake in Turing. Her advisors were focused elsewhere: trimming the Nvidia position in stages, opening a new stake in Berkshire Hathaway funded by that trim, and mapping out additions in healthcare and international exposure over the coming weeks. Intel, they say, is due for a decision. The waiting has gone on long enough.
