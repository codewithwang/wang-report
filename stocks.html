By Charmaine LoNews AnchorSun, Jul 19

Wall Street kept its composure, the majors flat and rate expectations parked exactly where the Fed left them. The tape offered no fireworks and, better still, no reason to panic. After last week, that counts as a gift.

The portfolio's technology bench had a mixed morning. Adobe extended its weekly climb on real momentum. Nvidia drew fresh competitive noise out of China aimed squarely at its software moat, a headline that will matter more in six months than it does today. Consumer names split hard. Amazon pressed its logistics ambitions against FedEx and UPS, unbothered as always by the idea that a competitor might stay a competitor. Lululemon slid to a fresh 52-week low, proof that even premium leggings answer to gravity. Okta drew analyst skepticism on valuation even while leaning into identity and AI, and Apple's Japan price hike is currency arithmetic wearing a strategy memo.

Her advisors have been busy. They are trimming the Nvidia position in stages over the coming weeks and redirecting early proceeds into a new stake in Berkshire Hathaway, with healthcare and international exposure eyed as the next builds. They have also drawn a hard line on the portfolio's smaller speculative names: no more adding, and automatic discipline on strength or on any break in thesis. Intel is a decision her advisors still owe her, not a position to hold indefinitely out of habit., CL
