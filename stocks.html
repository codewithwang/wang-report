By Charmaine LoNews AnchorWed, Jul 1

Wall Street closed out June without a headline number to lean on, the S&P, the Nasdaq and the Hang Seng all quiet on the tape, itself a kind of signal after a month spent arguing about rate cuts. Michael Burry's short against Nvidia and Tesla is the sentence traders keep forwarding to each other, less for the position size than for the worry it names, that the AI trade has priced perfection into chips nobody can audit. The rate mood, such as it is, remains a shrug dressed up as patience.

The technology book carries the portfolio's temperament today. Adobe's move on Topaz Labs reads as the sector's preferred answer to the Burry short, buy the picture-generation layer rather than defend the multiple, while Okta's jump on a stronger GF Score gives the software infrastructure sleeve a rare green morning. Consumer cyclical had its own vindication: Lululemon up on a resolved board fight, governance drama refunded once it clears. The leveraged semiconductor position remains the loudest way to hold a quiet opinion.

Nvidia is the name in focus, and the advisors have opened the trim, moving it toward a smaller share of the book in tranches over the coming weeks, with the first proceeds seeding a new Berkshire Hathaway position, a swap of momentum for ballast. Behind that, the advisors are sketching a healthcare allocation and an international sleeve for the weeks ahead, and have drafted exit rules for the portfolio's smaller, more speculative names. Intel sits on notice: a decision, not a delay.
