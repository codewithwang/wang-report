By Charmaine LoNews AnchorMon, Jul 6

Macro data was thin on the ground today, indices unreported at the close, which left the tape to trade on headlines instead of numbers. That suited the semiconductor half of this book fine. Intel logged another strong month, AMD and Intel have both outrun Nvidia over the first half of the year by return, and Hon Hai's upbeat AI demand read gave the whole chip complex a reason to feel good about itself, even the leveraged bet riding on top of it.

Technology remains the dominant force here, a concentrated book built around Apple, NVIDIA and their supporting cast. Chip names had the better day on the Hon Hai read-through. Adobe drew a bullish valuation call putting it meaningfully below fair value. Apple pressed ahead with folding-phone ambitions that will not matter to earnings for years. Consumer names moved on their own news: Lululemon slid further on doubts about its new chief executive, drawing in a well-known value investor precisely because everyone else was selling.

The advisors have been busy narrowing the book rather than expanding it. Their live conversation centers on trimming the largest technology position down in stages over the coming weeks, using the proceeds to open a position in Berkshire Hathaway, a rare vote for ballast in a portfolio otherwise leaning hard into growth. Healthcare and international exposure are next on the list, alongside firm exit rules for the more speculative names. Intel, they say, needs a decision, not a holding pattern.

So does everything else, eventually. Just not today.
