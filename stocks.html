By Charmaine LoNews AnchorWed, Jul 15

Amazon's recall of 67,000 coin batteries carries its own weight, but the tape today is broader than one item on a shelf. The Vanguard S&P 500 fund in this portfolio has climbed to 694, up nearly eleven percent this year, and that strength is concentrated in the same semiconductor names carrying this book. For now, the index and the portfolio are telling the same story.

That concentration cuts two ways this week. Nvidia is trading through a fresh round of export restrictions narrowing its list of approved Asia buyers, right as the advisors have discussed trimming that position down from its place as the largest holding in the book, selling in stages over the coming weeks rather than all at once. Okta, meanwhile, caught a lift from an unlikely source: an IBM warning on AI spending that somehow rallied the whole cybersecurity trade. Adobe took the opposite kind of headline, with BNP Paribas flagging AI tools eating into its core business, the sort of note that makes a software franchise sound like it is being disrupted by its own industry.

On individual names, the advisors have been just as busy behind the scenes as the tape has been in front of them. They've discussed opening a position in Berkshire Hathaway funded by that first Nvidia trim, adding some healthcare and international exposure in the months ahead, and writing strict rules for the smaller, more volatile names so discipline replaces hope. Intel remains a decision still waiting to be made. Even a portfolio can procrastinate.
