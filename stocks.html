By Charmaine LoNews AnchorSat, Jul 18

Amazon's billing engine spent the morning inventing sums no economy has ever produced, and somewhere in Seattle a very tired engineer is explaining to a customer why their AWS bill reads a trillion and a half dollars. Beyond that, the tape stayed a tape. The S&P and Nasdaq traded without histrionics, Hong Kong's market did the same, and rate mood held the quiet hum it's kept for weeks. No data shock, nothing forcing anyone's hand.

The portfolio's dominant technology book had its own small drama at the top. Apple retook the title of world's most valuable company, Nvidia clawed to hold the line, and the two traded the crown like a hallway trophy nobody actually wants to dust. Adobe was the standout, popping on an AI push into its Experience Cloud, while AMD sold off hard enough that analysts spent the day charting where the damage stops. Consumer names split: Amazon's headline was more comedy than crisis, but Lululemon took a real hit as Truist cut the stock to sell and flagged more room to fall. Okta got a vote of confidence with an upgrade at Capital One.

Her advisors have been talking succession, not panic. They're trimming Nvidia down from its outsized perch in stages over the coming weeks, using the first tranche to open a new stake in Berkshire Hathaway. Healthcare and international exposure are under discussion, hard rules are being written for the portfolio's speculative names, and Intel's fate, in the advisors' words, needs deciding one way or the other.

No more limbo.
