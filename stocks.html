By Charmaine LoNews AnchorWed, Jul 15

Portfolios traded quieter than the headlines today. Rate expectations held steady, indices firm without much conviction behind the move, and the tone across the tape was more digestion than direction. Nothing in the macro backdrop forced anyone's hand.

Technology carried the book's energy even on a calm day. Adobe's move into AI tooling and NVIDIA's continued access to Chinese demand both point the same direction, that the AI buildout keeps finding new legs whenever one seems to close. Okta's jump to a fresh high stood out in the software corner. Apple's regulatory clearance in China removes a lingering question mark over its device business there. Energy and the broad index sleeve traded more like ballast than headline, which on a day like this is exactly the job.

Individually, NVIDIA remains the portfolio's largest position, and the advisors have discussed trimming it down in stages over the coming weeks, using part of the proceeds to open a position in Berkshire Hathaway. They have also flagged adding some healthcare and international exposure over time, and separately want firm rules written for the portfolio's smallest, most volatile names before emotion writes them instead. Intel is due a decision, not another quarter of waiting.
