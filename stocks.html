By Charmaine LoNews AnchorTue, Jul 7

Bond markets did the talking today, since the macro calendar didn't offer usable levels, so the tape moved on positioning and headlines rather than the indices themselves. Amazon priced a large debt sale and told investors it's done borrowing for the year, a small signal that corporate treasurers still like conditions as they are. Cybersecurity had the better day of the two crosscurrents, rallying on analyst upgrades, while software security took a harder look after a severe flaw in Adobe's ColdFusion was confirmed under active attack.

Technology remains the portfolio's center of gravity, and that book had a mixed session under the surface. Chipmakers kept grinding higher, with AMD closing in on a trillion dollar market cap and Intel's ecosystem drawing fresh capital as a backed chipmaker filed for an IPO. Security software split the difference: CrowdStrike higher, Adobe under a cloud. Consumer names had their own story. Lululemon faces a lawsuit alleging tariff driven price increases never came back down after the tariffs eased, which is one way to find out how elastic loyalty really is.

Among individual names, Okta drew an upgrade and a higher price target from Scotiabank, a vote of confidence for the portfolio's infrastructure software holding. The advisors have been busier in strategy than in headlines this week. They trimmed the NVIDIA position in stages toward a smaller share of the book, funded a new Berkshire Hathaway stake from the first tranche, and are mapping out additions in healthcare and international exposure over the coming weeks. They have also set firm rules for the smaller, speculative names, and put Intel and the currency hedge question on the list for a decision, not a delay.
