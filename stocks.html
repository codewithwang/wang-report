By Charmaine LoNews AnchorWed, Jul 8

Amazon sold twenty-five billion dollars in bonds this week to fund its AI buildout, which tells you where the capital cycle stands even without a fresh print on the major indices today. Financing the future is easy. Pricing the present is another matter, and the chip complex spent the week proving it.

The portfolio's technology sleeve, its largest concentration by a wide margin, took the roughest weather. AMD fell eight percent on a Samsung-driven chip selloff, Intel dropped over twenty percent in a single month, and Nvidia slid on reports that DeepSeek is chasing a custom chip to route around US export limits. Software fared better. Okta jumped thirteen percent on an index shakeup and an AI security upgrade, while Adobe took a bearish call from BofA arguing that AI is eating its own growth. Consumer names were the calm room in the house: Lululemon opened its first India store, and Amazon's balance sheet did the heavy lifting everywhere else.

Her advisors have been busiest with Nvidia, discussing trimming it down from an outsized technology weighting in staged tranches over the coming weeks, with early proceeds seeding a new Berkshire Hathaway position. They're also mapping healthcare and international diversification for later this quarter, drafting hard exit rules for the portfolio's more speculative names, and weighing whether Intel deserves conviction or a clean exit.

Limbo is not on the list of acceptable outcomes.
