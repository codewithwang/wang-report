By Charmaine LoNews AnchorSat, Jul 11

Amazon and Apple carried the headline load today, and neither in a way that flattered the corner office. Amazon's social casino apps just opened a $200 million settlement door, and Apple is now in court accusing OpenAI of trade secret theft, which is one way to make Silicon Valley's favorite legal document a lawsuit. The S&P 500, Nasdaq, and Hang Seng traded without a data feed today, a silence the market bore with more grace than most vendors would.

Technology remains the portfolio's largest and most concentrated book, and it had a mixed session by any measure. AMD rallied on a new AI infrastructure alliance, NVIDIA's top RAM supplier debuted on Wall Street at a trillion dollars, and Adobe pushed further into generative video with its Rephrase.AI purchase. Intel sat under fresh scrutiny over its turnaround plan, Okta slid on an index exit and valuation worries, and Lululemon took a hit after Jefferies said the damage there is done. The leveraged semiconductor sleeve and the broad index fund moved with the sector's temper, for better and for worse.

Her advisors have been busy. The near-term plan trims NVIDIA down to a smaller slice of the technology book in staged tranches, with proceeds seeding a new Berkshire Hathaway position, while healthcare and international diversification sit queued behind it. They are also drafting hard exit rules for SoundHound and Unusual Machines: no adds, half off on a double, full exit if the thesis breaks. Intel, meanwhile, has been told to stop sitting in limbo.
