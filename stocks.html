Macro data was thin on the tape today, but the mood was not. Rates chatter dominated, and the index flows told their own story better than any headline did. Vanguard's S&P 500 fund became the first ETF in history to cross a trillion dollars, which says less about Vanguard and more about a decade of investors deciding not to pick stocks at all.

Technology stayed the story inside this book, and the sector had a mixed session. Adobe drew attention on a call that it may be meaningfully undervalued as AI features roll into its core products. NVIDIA faced fresh noise out of China over a rival chip claiming an enormous speed advantage, the kind of headline built to move sentiment before it moves a single shipping schedule. AMD had the better week, riding one of its strongest stretches in years. Consumer names were mixed too: Amazon featured in a routine settlement story, while Lululemon slid on a Texas inquiry into chemicals in its fabrics.

In the largest position, the advisors have discussed trimming NVIDIA down from its outsized place in the technology book, gradually, in tranches over the coming weeks, with proceeds earmarked to open a new stake in Berkshire Hathaway. Beyond that, the conversation has turned to diversification: adding healthcare exposure, building out international holdings, and setting firm rules for the more speculative names before the next big swing, not after it. Intel is due a decision. Patience has had its turn., CL
