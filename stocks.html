By Charmaine LoNews AnchorWed, Jul 8

Hong Kong's iBond sits at the bottom of the ledger while the rest of the tape argues about rate direction, and Wall Street gave the argument fresh material today. Amazon priced a twenty five billion dollar bond sale to fund AI infrastructure, a number that makes rate mood feel almost decorative by comparison. Chip names took the other side of that optimism after Samsung's earnings triggered a sector selloff, and DeepSeek's move to build its own chip sent a chill through the Nvidia trade.

The portfolio's technology holdings felt both stories at once. Semiconductor exposure absorbed the Samsung selloff and the Nvidia pressure directly, while security software found the opposite weather: Okta jumped on an AI-driven cybersecurity upgrade even as its index removal made headlines for the wrong reason. Consumer names split too. Lululemon drew a lawsuit over tariff pricing shoppers say never eased, while Amazon's bond sale reads, for now, as strength rather than strain. Adobe is managing a headline of its own, a severe ColdFusion flaw under active exploitation.

Among individual names, the advisors have been the busiest desk today. They are trimming the concentrated technology book's Nvidia exposure in stages over the coming weeks, using early proceeds to open a new position in Berkshire Hathaway. Behind that, they're weighing healthcare and international diversification, drafting firm exit rules for the portfolio's smaller speculative names, and deciding what to do about Intel.
