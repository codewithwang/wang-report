The Numbers Running on Different Clocks

Goldman at fifteen, twenty-four aircraft, AWS keys on GitHub, two hundred dead in the Philippines: a week of correct numbers that were not in conversation.
Charmaine Lo · News Anchor

The Goldman revision, fifteen percent, appeared in the estate agent windows on Friday, printed on A4, some laminated. The authority processing the city's backlog of 110,000 illegal flats had certified four units in the same period, and the fatal Yau Ma Tei fire this week killed residents in a building whose neighbors hold unregistered title on the same asset Goldman is pricing as upside -- a 5.9 percent Q1 recovery the IMF endorsed but that has not reached the credit windows and minimum-order thresholds where those owners and the district's small operators run their calculations. The gazette published new civil service investigation regulations on the same Friday, handing two very different papers to two very different readers. The Goldman revision and the gazette note arrived the same morning. They were not addressed to anyone in common. -- CL

Twenty-four PLA aircraft crossed the median line on Tuesday. The Xi-Trump summit had produced language about military trust as the foundation of bilateral progress the same week, which is not contradiction but design: the summit converts Taiwan arms transfers from a US defense obligation into a trade concession, and the aircraft crossing the median line that same Tuesday demonstrate what deterrence costs now that the obligation carries a negotiated price, which is exactly the variable Taiwan's defense planners are now running with a value Beijing controls. Taipei has both documents. The sortie count is the operative signal going into the NDAA markup; the communique is the frame around it. -- CL

Southbound Connect flows ran steady into HKEX last week. The equity desks read that as mainland confidence in H-shares, but the capital-account mechanics behind those flows price RMB depreciation risk; SAFE administers those mechanics, and the SFC's family-office conduct data this week showed registration counts and deployed capital pointing at separate answers: two official counts of the same money that do not agree. OpenAI pressed ChatGPT into bank-account integration in the same five days while its chief executive's credibility is formally contested in US federal court, and the enforcement framework large enough to contain the liability surface that creates is American tort law, which was not designed for this product at this scale. The capital is already inside the account. The regulation is still being written. -- CL

A CISA contractor left AWS access keys on GitHub this week. The contractor works for the agency that sets remediation timelines for the rest of the US federal government, which is one of those institutional facts that does not require annotation. Two CVSS 10 Cisco SD-WAN vulnerabilities were exploited within five months of each other, a JavaScript supply chain burned two OpenAI developer devices, and the pattern across all three is the same: the window between public disclosure and active exploitation has closed to a length that standard patching cycles from a different decade cannot meet. The keys went up on a Tuesday. The audit starts after. -- CL

Kalmaegi reached the Philippines on Tuesday and killed nearly two hundred people. The cat bond market cleared $68.5 billion in Q1, a record, almost none of it written against western Pacific typhoon exposure, because the premium arithmetic on US wind and hail perils is more favorable; Munich Re is forecasting rising typhoon frequency for this ocean, the capital is in the other ocean, and the people in Kalmaegi's path had access to neither the capital nor the forecast that would have priced them into coverage. The protection gap is a product design decision, not a capital shortage. Watch the NDAA markup on Taiwan arms the week of June 2nd -- it will tell you whether the Beijing summit reset the deterrence calculation or simply gave the new terms a name. -- CL
