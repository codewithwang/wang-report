Goldman's Fifteen and the Aircraft Count

The week Hong Kong's recovery distributed its evidence unevenly, twenty-four aircraft said what the summit would not, and a Tuesday product launch found no regulator.
Charmaine Lo · News Anchor

Estate agent windows in Wan Chai had new numbers taped in on Friday. Goldman's fifteen percent upward revision for residential prices arrived the same morning as the civil service investigation regulation in the Gazette, and the two documents traveled the same newspaper cycle without ever addressing the same reader -- not because they contradicted each other, but because they were written for people who do not share a waiting room. One paper said the market had come back. The other said who would be watched while it did. -- CL

Four units certified from a backlog of 110,000 illegal subdivided flats, in a year when secondary prices are forecast to rise eleven percent. The owners of the other 109,996 are watching an estate agent tape Goldman's numbers to a window their paperwork cannot open. The Southbound Stock Connect flows that read as mainland equity confidence are priced on RMB depreciation risk, not H-share valuations -- the buyers are running a different calculation than the inbound pitch deck implies. The family office count that leads every presentation to a financial regulator is pointing in a different direction than the SFC's conduct data. The 5.9 first-quarter growth figure is real, IMF-endorsed, not disputed. It has not reached the credit windows and minimum-order thresholds where small operators actually run their numbers. -- CL

The Xi-Trump summit closed with a statement about military trust. Twenty-four PLA aircraft flew against Taiwan the same week -- the sortie count is what institutions price cross-strait risk on, not the communique. Xi told Trump directly that the Thucydides Trap applies, which is a warning dressed as a seminar reference. The NDAA markup arrived in Washington before the deal was finished, and what it settled is that Taiwan arms transfers are no longer a US defense obligation; they are a concession Beijing can move in trade negotiations. Tulsi Gabbard left the intelligence directorship on Friday. The summit photograph was warm. Taiwan's defense planners are reading a different document. -- CL

The contractor who left AWS access keys in a public GitHub repository works for the US agency that sets federal remediation timelines for the class of breach those keys enabled. The same five days produced two CVSS 10 Cisco SD-WAN exploits, both actively used within five months of disclosure, and a JavaScript supply chain attack that burned two OpenAI developer devices. The pattern is structural: the exploitation window has closed before the patch window opens. The credential sitting in version control is the same type of credential that was supposed to prevent the next one. -- CL

Greg Brockman returned to OpenAI product. ChatGPT pressed into bank-account integration. The CEO's credibility was formally contested in federal court in the same week the banking feature launched, and no US enforcement framework -- not the OCC, not the CFPB, not any pending statute with agreed text -- covers the liability surface OpenAI has now claimed. The constraint is tort law. The feature launched on a Tuesday. -- CL

Next week, the Iran ceasefire mediators are in the room with a clock that belongs to neither side, and the arms freeze Washington applied to Taiwan in exchange for trade leverage remains on the ledger for whatever is being traded there. The cat bond market cleared $68.5 billion in the first quarter on almost entirely American perils; Kalmaegi, which killed nearly two hundred people in the Philippines this week while that record held, will not be the last typhoon to test the gap between the western Pacific's weather and the market designed to price it. The first sign of whether the Taiwan variable moves will come before the weekend. -- CL
