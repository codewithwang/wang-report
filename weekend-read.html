Goldman's Fifteen, Beijing's Twenty-Four

Goldman's fifteen percent, Beijing's sortie count, and a contractor's credentials in plain view: the week's signals were never hidden.
Charmaine Lo · News Anchor

Goldman's fifteen percent revision hit the estate agent windows on Hennessy Road at noon on Friday, the same afternoon the civil service investigation regulation landed in the Gazette, two documents addressed to two very different readers who shared the same commute home. The owners of the 110,000 illegal flats that the Buildings Department has certified at a running total of four are watching a price recovery they can read through glass and not yet reach through the paperwork; the certification pace turns fifteen percent into a view before it becomes a close, and the secondary market has been rising since January without disturbing the stack of unregistered paper beneath it. Four units certified. One hundred and ten thousand still in the queue. The number in the window is real. -- CL

Twenty-four PLA aircraft flew against Taiwan in summit week, when Beijing was describing the Xi-Trump sessions as a restoration of strategic trust between the two governments. The US confirmation that arms sales to Taipei are now paused arrived inside the same news cycle that converted a defense obligation into a trade concession at the table, which means Taipei's planners are now pricing deterrence against a variable that Washington can move between meetings without a vote. The sortie count is what institutions use to price cross-strait risk. The concession was not in their model until this week. -- CL

A contractor's AWS keys were in a GitHub repository while the agency that employs him managed the federal remediation timelines for breaches of this kind. Two CVSS 10 Cisco SD-WAN vulnerabilities reached exploitation within five months of disclosure, a JavaScript supply chain attack burned two OpenAI developer devices in the same week, and the sequence argues that the window between disclosure and live exploitation has closed structurally rather than narrowed. OpenAI's answer to that environment was to push ChatGPT into bank-account integration, with its CEO's credibility under formal federal contest and no assigned US regulator for the liability surface. Greg Brockman returned to product. The product now asks for your bank login. -- CL

The SFC's family-office conduct register runs at a different number from the headline count of 2,700 registered offices, and both numbers are appearing in the same inbound pitch decks this week without acknowledgment of the gap. The Southbound Connect flows that every desk summary reads as mainland equity confidence are, at the capital-account level, a mechanism for pricing RMB depreciation risk rather than H-share valuations; the buyers and the headlines describe separate positions. Hong Kong's 5.9 percent Q1 growth arrived IMF-endorsed and has not reached the credit windows and minimum-order thresholds where small operators work out whether the week is viable. The registry says 2,700. The conduct data runs differently. -- CL

Kalmaegi's death toll reached nearly 200 in the Philippines in the same week the catastrophe bond market cleared ten billion dollars. The Q1 cat bond record of $68.5 billion ran on US perils almost entirely; Munich Re's forecast of rising typhoon frequency in the western Pacific puts the capital and the next storm track on different sides of the dateline. The protection gap is not a money problem. It is a product that was never built for the ocean where the frequency is rising. -- CL

The Supreme Court takes up Mahmoud Khalil's deportation case next week, and the ruling will arrive inside the same enforcement logic that already requires Green Card applicants to leave the United States to file. -- CL
