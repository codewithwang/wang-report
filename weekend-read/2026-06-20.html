Three Thousand Kilometers and Ten Days

From Wang Fuk Court's June 30 cutoff to Beijing's three-thousand-kilometer carrier doctrine, the week arrived at its numbers with hard edges and soft institutional answers.
Charmaine Lo · News Anchor

The playgrounds in Sha Tin opened at eight Saturday morning, before the heat settled in. Two infants were in the paediatric ICU at Prince of Wales Hospital by then, both unvaccinated, both admitted during a week when district health authorities warned in plain language that COVID transmission would rise over the next two months and asked parents to verify children's immunisation records before the school summer break. The venues opened. The ferry piers opened. The recommendation for unvaccinated children under six was phrased as a recommendation, and the weekend programme did not adjust. -- CL

Wang Fuk Court's buyback cutoff is June 30, ten days from this Saturday. Hop On Management missed two court-upheld deadlines to convene the owners meeting that would let 1,984 displaced households collectively examine the terms before that date closes -- which means those households arrive at June 30 without the review process the redevelopment law created for exactly this situation, and without which a collective decision becomes a series of individual ones made in the dark. Both courts ruled clearly. Both deadlines passed. The clock is still running. The meeting has not been called. -- CL

Washington paused fourteen billion dollars in Taiwan arms this week. Beijing, in the same week, named three thousand kilometers as the strike range of its carrier doctrine -- the distance to Guam -- and sanctioned Eduardo Teodoro in a move timed alongside the doctrine disclosure rather than announced separately from it. The target is a legislature whose calendar is occupied and an executive whose news cycle has moved on from this file. Coercive timing requires the recipient to be looking at something else when the message arrives, and Washington was: a domestic spending debate, a ceasefire in a different theater, a football tournament in another country's cities. The arms pipeline is paused. The doctrine has a number. The number is three thousand kilometers. -- CL

Sygnia's Operation Highland found nine backdoored Linux authentication modules running undetected for ten years inside financial infrastructure. MAS TRM requires institutions to notify the regulator within one hour of a significant breach. The one-hour clock cannot start until the institution knows the intrusion exists, and this class of intrusion is built to prevent that knowledge from forming; an authentication stack compromised in 2016 and discovered in 2026 did not fail to report within one hour, it failed to know for ten years -- which is a different failure and one the clock was not written to measure. The framework is not wrong. It was written for a different attack. This one ran for a decade. -- CL

Fable 5 was suspended this week after government regulators cited a jailbreak that Anthropic had disclosed. The disclosure is what gave regulators the specific citation they needed to act. OpenAI documented the identical technique in GPT-5.5 and did not disclose it, and GPT-5.5 has not been suspended. What this writes into the record for the next company that finds a comparable vulnerability is now legible in the outcome: disclosure produces a regulatory citation; silence does not. Whether that lesson, as it travels through boardrooms and legal teams over the next several months, produces better security outcomes or worse outcomes for the systems the public uses is the question the week left open. The near-term one, it answered. -- CL

The ceasefire tracked by wire services held through Friday. The parties have until early next week to decide whether that sentence still applies, and whatever they decide will set the frame for Monday's edition. The Wang Fuk Court households have until June 30. Both calendars are running. -- CL
