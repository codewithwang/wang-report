Fifteen Percent and Twenty-Four Aircraft

Goldman's revision went into estate agent windows; Beijing's sortie count went into risk models; both arrived this week, and the same reader needs both.
Charmaine Lo · News Anchor

Queen's Road East estate agent windows carried Goldman's number by Friday: a fifteen percent upward revision to secondary prices for the year. The civil service investigation regulation arrived in the same Gazette, and whoever opened the one had no cause to open the other, because the revision belongs to the recovery story and the regulation belongs to the governance story, and Hong Kong has learned to file both on the same Friday morning without expecting either to explain the other. Four units certified from a backlog of 110,000 illegal flats. Q1 GDP came in at 5.9 percent, IMF-endorsed and politically settled. The credit windows where small operators calculate minimum orders have not updated on the same schedule. The owners holding unregistered paper are watching a recovery they cannot access. -- CL

Twenty-four PLA aircraft logged against Taiwan during the same days the Xi-Trump bilateral was running, the same days the communiques circulated about productive atmospheres and structural trust. The summit converted Taiwan arms transfers from a US defense obligation into a trade concession, which is a different document than a joint statement; the NDAA markup arrived in Washington before the deal closed, and that sequencing is how Taipei's institutions discovered the variable had moved before anyone said it publicly. Models price sorties, not atmospheres. The count this week was twenty-four. The median line held. -- CL

Two studies on sleep landed this week and neither has reached the protocol clinic billing cycle. A large-cohort analysis found sleep-timing regularity, when you sleep rather than how long, outperforms duration as an all-cause mortality predictor; the APAC precision epigenetics panels charge for a different variable, and the billing rate did not adjust this week. The grey-market peptide stack prices irisin-pathway exposure at a significant premium without Phase 1 data filed anywhere; two studies showed resistance training produces the same molecule with the same measurable clinical endpoints and no minimum order. Same outcome. No invoice. -- CL

The contractor who left AWS access keys on a public GitHub repository works for CISA, the agency that sets remediation timelines for US federal systems. Two CVSS 10 Cisco SD-WAN vulnerabilities were exploited in five months; a JavaScript supply chain compromise burned two OpenAI developer devices in the same reporting week; and OpenAI pressed bank-account integration into ChatGPT past any US enforcement framework built to contain it, its CEO's credibility formally contested in federal court on the same news cycle. The exploitation window the industry built its patch timelines around has structurally closed. The oversight gap is not pending legislation. US tort law is the operative constraint, and the lawyers are already billing. -- CL

Kalmaegi tracked through the Philippines this week and killed nearly two hundred people, and the catastrophe bond market cleared ten billion dollars in the same period. Q1 2026's record $68.5 billion in cat bonds ran almost entirely on US perils while Munich Re forecast rising typhoon frequency in the western Pacific, pulling capital and risk to opposite sides of the map, sorted by peril geography rather than by where people live in the path of the peril. The protection gap is not a capital shortage. The capital cleared $68.5 billion in a single quarter. It is pointed at different oceans. -- CL

Rubio briefs India on the Xi-Trump talks before the end of next week, and that read-out goes to a government that needs to know whether the arms-concession version or the defense-obligation version of the Taiwan equation is operative before the next sortie count runs. -- CL
