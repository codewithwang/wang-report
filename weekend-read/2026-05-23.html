The Record and the Claim

A week when the sortie count, the certification backlog, and the capital-account mechanics each told a different story from the headline above them.
Charmaine Lo · News Anchor

The estate agent window in Causeway Bay changed its numbers on Friday afternoon. Goldman's fifteen-percent upward revision landed on the same day the government published the civil service investigation regulation in the Gazette, and the two documents found different readers in the same streets: the flat-owner watching secondary prices recover from a position he cannot access, and the civil servant reading the new accountability language for the first time. Four units received certification from the regularization program this week, against a declared backlog of 110,000. Q1 growth came in at 5.9%, IMF-endorsed, and it will appear in the budget defense for years. The owners of the 109,996 uncertified flats, and the small operators running their numbers at the credit window, are watching a recovery at some distance from it. -- CL

Twenty-four PLA aircraft crossed the Taiwan median line during the week Beijing was describing the Xi-Trump summit as a trust-restoration exercise. The sortie count is the counter-argument filed concurrent with the communique, and it is the number that institutions pricing cross-strait risk read over the weekend while the diplomatic framing held the headlines. What the summit produced in operational terms is a variable Beijing can now move: Taiwan arms transfers have been converted from a US defense obligation into a trade instrument, which means Taipei's defense planning incorporates a number Washington can adjust in the next round. The NDAA markup arrived in Congress before the deal terms were settled. Taipei reads the sortie count and the NDAA timetable together, and the planning calculus from those two documents is not the one that came out of the summit communique. -- CL

The CISA contractor left AWS credentials in a public GitHub repository. The agency whose mandate covers federal remediation timelines spent the week explaining that this particular key exposure was not its direct responsibility. Two CVSS 10 Cisco SD-WAN vulnerabilities were exploited within five months of each other; a JavaScript supply chain compromise hit two OpenAI developer devices; none of these required zero-days. They required credentials left where someone would eventually look, and exploitation windows that closed before the advisories finished circulating. The CISA breach and the Cisco timeline point at something structural: the window between disclosure and exploitation has closed. The same week, OpenAI pushed ChatGPT into bank-account integration with no single US enforcement framework owning the liability surface. The federal banking regulators, the FTC, and the existing tort system are each describing the same problem from jurisdictions that do not overlap and cannot be made to. Greg Brockman returned to OpenAI product this week. -- CL

Southbound Connect flows into HKEX read as mainland equity confidence; the capital-account mechanics below the headline price RMB depreciation, not H-share valuations. The family-office registration count leads every inbound pitch deck in Hong Kong, and the SFC conduct data on deployed capital is pointing in a different direction. Across the Pacific, the cat bond market cleared $10 billion while Typhoon Kalmaegi killed nearly 200 people in the Philippines; the protection gap between what western Pacific risk needs priced and what the capital market is pricing is a product design failure, not a shortage of funds, and Munich Re's typhoon frequency forecast for the region pulled capital and risk further apart through the week. A large-cohort study published this week found that sleep-timing regularity outperforms total sleep duration as an all-cause mortality predictor; the APAC protocol clinic market prices epigenetic precision on a different variable. Kakao paid $1 billion for SM Entertainment. The trainee contract is now the asset. -- CL

The Supreme Court takes up the jurisdictional question in Mahmoud Khalil's deportation case next week, and the answer will be read in consulates from Hong Kong to Manila as a signal about the outer boundary of what removal can cover. -- CL
