Gold overtakes US Treasuries as world's largest reserve asset, ECB data shows
The ECB reported that gold has displaced US Treasuries as the single largest component of global central bank reserves, reaching a 27% share following a historic bullion rally. The shift reflects sustained de-dollarisation by central banks and marks a structural change in how sovereign wealth is stored.
WHY TODAY: ECB published new reserve composition data showing gold has crossed the 27% threshold, formally surpassing Treasuries for the first time on record.

Red Hat npm packages compromised in supply chain attack stealing developer credentials
Attackers used a hijacked GitHub account to inject credential-stealing malware into 32 Red Hat cloud-services packages downloaded roughly 117,000 times per week. Red Hat has removed the tainted packages, but developer credentials from affected environments may already be exfiltrated.
WHY TODAY: Red Hat disclosed and began remediation today after preliminary analysis confirmed the compromised GitHub account as the supply chain entry point, with a new Miasma malware variant identified.

NIST vulnerability database backlog doubles to 27,000 entries, declared ineffective by inspector general
An inspector general report found NIST's National Vulnerability Database accumulated over 27,000 unprocessed vulnerability records by end of 2025, up from 13,000 in early 2024, severely degrading the tool security teams worldwide use for risk prioritisation. The failure leaves a critical gap in global cyber infrastructure at a time of rising threat activity.
WHY TODAY: The inspector general's report was released today, formally documenting that the NVD backlog has more than doubled and has undermined the database's utility and public trust.
