Pope Leo Issues First Papal Document on AI, Calls for Global Regulation and Shared Control
Pope Leo XIV released the first major theological document of his pontificate, calling for robust international regulation of artificial intelligence and warning that control of the technology must not remain in the hands of a few actors. WHY TODAY: The encyclical was published today, marking the first time a pope has formally addressed AI governance as a matter of Catholic doctrine, adding significant institutional weight to calls for binding international rules.

The platform industrialises enterprise credential theft, lowering the skill barrier for attackers targeting organisations that rely on Microsoft cloud infrastructure.
WHY TODAY: The FBI issued its formal advisory today, elevating Kali365 from a known threat to an official priority warning that security teams at Microsoft-dependent organisations must now act on.
