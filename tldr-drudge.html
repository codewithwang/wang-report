Trump threatens to strike ally Oman if it backs Iran over Strait of Hormuz access

ECB vice-president warns Trump trade policies could trigger a global financial crisis
European Central Bank vice-president Luis de Guindos said Washington's volatile trade stance and retreat from multilateral cooperation pose systemic risk to global financial stability. The warning from a sitting central banker adds institutional weight to concerns previously voiced mainly by markets and economists.
WHY TODAY: A serving ECB official directly named Trump policy as a potential financial crisis trigger today, marking a sharper public tone from European monetary authorities.

China uses electronic warfare to expel Dutch navy vessel near disputed Paracel Islands
The PLA Southern Theatre Command disclosed it deployed electronic interference measures against a Dutch warship near the Paracel Islands and accused the Dutch navy of risking military miscalculation. The public acknowledgment of electronic warfare against a NATO member vessel in the South China Sea is a rare and deliberate escalation signal.
WHY TODAY: China openly acknowledged electronic interference against a European navy ship today, stepping beyond its usual silence on such tactics.

FBI warns extortion hackers are physically visiting US law firms to steal data
The FBI issued a formal advisory warning that the Silent Ransom Group is targeting law firms with in-person social engineering visits to gain remote access and exfiltrate sensitive client data. The shift from purely remote intrusion to physical presence at targets marks a significant tactical escalation for the extortion threat landscape.
WHY TODAY: The FBI published a public advisory today for the first time about in-person attacks on law firms, formalizing a novel physical component to this threat actor.

CrowdStrike and Google dismantle Glassworm botnet used in software supply-chain attacks
Researchers disrupted the Glassworm botnet, which infected open source projects to compromise downstream developers and enterprises, after taking down a resilient command-and-control infrastructure built on Solana blockchain transactions and BitTorrent. The supply-chain targeting model makes the campaign significant beyond its immediate victim count.
WHY TODAY: The coordinated takedown of Glassworm's blockchain-based infrastructure was announced today, ending an active campaign that had evaded conventional disruption methods.

The attribution adds US critical transit infrastructure to Iran's active targeting portfolio alongside its conventional military conflict with Washington.
WHY TODAY: Formal state attribution to Iranian intelligence was published today, elevating a previously ambiguous incident to confirmed nation-state level.
