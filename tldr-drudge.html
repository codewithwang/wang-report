Nasdaq Drops 4% as Bumper US Jobs Report Stokes Federal Reserve Rate Rise Fears
US employers added 172,000 jobs in May, nearly double analyst expectations, pushing investors to reprice Federal Reserve rate expectations sharply higher and sending the Nasdaq to its largest single-day fall since early 2025, with chip and memory stocks bearing the heaviest losses. The combination of a resilient labour market and rising bond yields threatens the valuation assumptions underpinning the AI-driven equity rally that has dominated markets this year.
WHY TODAY: The May jobs report was released today, coming in at 172,000 versus a 90,000 consensus, triggering immediate bond yield spikes and a broad tech selloff.

04. BUSINESS & DEALS ●●●●○
Google Signs 30 Billion Dollar Compute Deal with SpaceX Ahead of Expected IPO
Google has agreed to pay SpaceX approximately 920 million dollars per month for computing capacity, a deal worth roughly 30 billion dollars driven by unexpected demand for Google's recently launched AI products. The agreement concentrates a significant share of the world's AI compute procurement with a single pre-IPO company and deepens structural dependencies between two of the most strategically consequential firms in the AI infrastructure race.
WHY TODAY: The financial terms of the deal were disclosed today, revealing the full scale of Google's compute dependency on SpaceX infrastructure for the first time.

EU Launches Chips Act 2.0 and Cloud Law to Cut Reliance on US and Chinese Tech
The European Commission unveiled a technology sovereignty package bundling a new Chips Act, a Cloud and AI Development Act, an open source strategy, and a digital energy roadmap, all aimed at reducing European dependence on US and Chinese suppliers. The legislation represents the EU's most comprehensive attempt to secure its own digital stack and positions Brussels as an active third pole in the global AI and semiconductor competition.
WHY TODAY: The full package was formally published today, converting months of anticipation into concrete draft legislation subject to parliamentary process.

Chinese Espionage Group Deploys Previously Unknown Malware to Persist Inside Microsoft 365 Environments

The disclosure reveals a more sophisticated and previously invisible layer of Chinese cyber-espionage tooling that defenders across APAC and Western enterprises must now account for.
WHY TODAY: Researchers published details of the new malware strains today, making Plenet and AgentPSD known to defenders for the first time.

10. SIGNAL ●●●○○
First Vaccine with Fully AI-Designed Active Ingredient Completes Human Safety Trial
A small early-phase human trial of a broad-spectrum vaccine whose active ingredient was designed entirely by artificial intelligence showed a modest but measurable immune response, according to a newly published study. The result, while preliminary, is the first human data validating AI-driven de novo vaccine design and opens a research pathway toward protection against multiple viruses from a single candidate.
WHY TODAY: The trial results were published today, providing the first reported human immune-response data for a fully AI-designed vaccine.
