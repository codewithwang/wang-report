01. GEOPOLITICS ●●●●●
Trump threatens Oman with attack as Iran nuclear deal talks remain deadlocked
President Trump warned Oman it would be 'blown up' if it sides with Iran over control of the Strait of Hormuz, even as US-Iran negotiations remain deadlocked over terms both sides reject. The threat against a key Gulf mediator adds a new escalation vector to a conflict already disrupting global energy markets and oil pricing.

02. APAC ●●●●○
China publicly confirms using electronic warfare to expel Dutch warship from Paracel Islands
China's PLA Southern Theatre Command acknowledged deploying electronic interference to drive away a Dutch naval vessel near the disputed Paracel Islands, accusing the Dutch navy of risking 'miscalculation.' The unusually candid admission signals Beijing is willing to openly advertise electronic warfare use against NATO member navies operating in contested South China Sea waters.
WHY TODAY: The PLA's public statement confirming electronic interference against a European warship in the South China Sea was released Wednesday, a rare explicit disclosure of EW deployment.

03. GEOPOLITICS ●●●●○
Israel kills newly appointed Hamas military wing chief in Gaza City airstrike
An Israeli strike in Gaza City killed Mohammed Odeh, the recently installed head of Hamas's military wing, along with his wife and two children. Eliminating Hamas's military leadership replacement so rapidly deepens the humanitarian toll and complicates any near-term ceasefire or hostage deal trajectory.
WHY TODAY: Hamas confirmed Odeh's death Wednesday. He had only recently been appointed to replace a previously killed commander, making this a same-generation targeted killing.
SOURCES: BBC World  •  READ →
04. MARKETS & MACRO ●●●●○
ECB vice president warns Trump trade policies risk triggering a global financial crisis
European Central Bank Vice President Luis de Guindos stated that Washington's volatile trade posture and reduced international cooperation pose a material threat to global financial stability. A formal warning from a major central bank elevates the policy risk premium on dollar assets and signals Europe is preparing contingencies for a US-driven financial shock.
WHY TODAY: De Guindos made the statement public Wednesday, the first ECB formulation explicitly naming Trump's trade policy as a financial crisis trigger rather than a growth drag.
SOURCES: FT  •  READ →
05. CYBERSECURITY ●●●○○
FBI warns extortion gang is physically visiting US law firms to steal client data
The FBI issued a public advisory Tuesday warning that the Silent Ransom Group is conducting in-person social engineering attacks at US law firm offices to gain remote system access and exfiltrate sensitive client files. The tactic marks an escalation from purely digital intrusion to physical presence at targeted offices, raising the threat profile for professional services firms holding privileged communications.
WHY TODAY: The FBI advisory was issued Tuesday and represents the first public warning of in-person attacks by this group specifically targeting law firms.
SOURCES: The Record, BleepingComputer  •  READ →
06. CYBERSECURITY ●●●○○
Glassworm supply chain botnet dismantled after targeting open source software developers
CrowdStrike and Google researchers disrupted the Glassworm botnet, which used Solana blockchain transactions and BitTorrent DHT to maintain resilient command-and-control infrastructure while infecting open source projects and compromising downstream developers and enterprises. The takedown is notable because the botnet weaponized the software supply chain itself, not just individual endpoints.
WHY TODAY: The takedown was announced Wednesday by researchers and confirmed by CrowdStrike and Google, concluding an operation against one of the more technically sophisticated supply-chain attack infrastructures documented to date.
SOURCES: BleepingComputer, TechCrunch  •  READ →
07. CYBERSECURITY ●●●○○
Iran's Ministry of Intelligence attributed to cyberattack on Los Angeles transit system
Researchers at Gambit Security published a report attributing the hack on the Los Angeles transit system to a group with direct ties to Iran's Ministry of Intelligence, contradicting its self-presentation as an independent hacktivist crew. The attribution confirms Iran is operating through front organizations to conduct infrastructure attacks, blurring the line between state-sponsored and criminal cyber operations.
WHY TODAY: The Gambit Security attribution report was published Tuesday, providing the first confirmed state-linked attribution for the LA transit hack.
SOURCES: The Record  •  READ →
08. AI & TECH ●●●○○
AI coding startup Cognition raises $1 billion at $25 billion valuation
Cognition, maker of the AI software developer Devin, closed a $1 billion funding round at a $25 billion pre-money valuation after reporting $492 million in annualized revenue, more than doubling its valuation in eight months. The round sets a new benchmark for AI-native development tools and intensifies pressure on incumbents across the software developer toolchain.
WHY TODAY: The round and valuation were announced Wednesday, representing the largest single funding event for an AI coding tool to date.
SOURCES: TechCrunch  •  READ →

JPMorgan CEO signals bank could spend up to $20 billion on an acquisition
Jamie Dimon said Trump's lighter regulatory approach has freed as much as $50 billion in excess capital at JPMorgan, and the bank could deploy up to $20 billion on a major acquisition. The statement is the clearest signal yet that the largest US bank is actively scoping a deal that would reshape competitive dynamics across investment banking and retail finance.
WHY TODAY: Dimon made the comments Wednesday and for the first time quantified a specific acquisition budget figure publicly.
SOURCES: FT  •  READ →
10. REGULATION & POLICY ●●●○○
UK visa portal exposed passport scans and biometric selfies of thousands of applicants
A third-party website handling UK visa applications exposed passports, biometric selfies, and location data of thousands of applicants, and responded to responsible disclosure by sending lawyers rather than remedying the breach. The incident illustrates systemic risk in outsourcing government identity verification to inadequately secured third parties, with implications for similar programs across multiple jurisdictions.
WHY TODAY: TechCrunch published its investigation Wednesday, revealing the data remained exposed and that legal threats were deployed to suppress disclosure rather than fix the vulnerability.
