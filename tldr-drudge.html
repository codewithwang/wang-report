01. GEOPOLITICS ●●●●○
Trump threatens military action against Oman over Strait of Hormuz stance
US President Trump warned that Oman must behave or face attack after being asked whether he would accept Oman and Iran jointly controlling the Strait of Hormuz. The threat places a key US Gulf ally in an impossible diplomatic position and injects fresh risk into one of the world's most critical energy chokepoints.
WHY TODAY: Trump named Oman explicitly for the first time in the Hormuz context on Wednesday, converting a negotiating subtext into a direct public threat against an ally.

02. APAC ●●●●○
China uses electronic warfare to expel Dutch warship near Paracel Islands
China's PLA Southern Theatre Command acknowledged deploying electronic interference to drive off a Dutch navy vessel near the disputed Paracel Islands, unusually warning that Dutch actions risked miscalculation. The admission marks the first public Chinese confirmation of using electronic measures against a NATO member's ship in the South China Sea.
WHY TODAY: The PLA issued its statement Wednesday, making the electronic warfare deployment the first explicitly acknowledged instance against a European navy vessel in the SCS.

03. GEOPOLITICS ●●●●○
Israeli airstrike kills newly appointed head of Hamas military wing in Gaza City
Hamas confirmed that Mohammed Odeh, recently installed as head of its military wing, was killed in an Israeli strike on a residential building in Gaza City along with his wife and two children. The rapid killing of a newly appointed commander signals Israel's sustained capacity to locate Hamas leadership even as the group reconstitutes its chain of command.
WHY TODAY: Odeh had only recently taken the role, making Wednesday's strike a direct response to the appointment rather than targeting a long-established figure.

Norway to join France's nuclear umbrella in formal shift away from US security reliance
France and Norway announced Oslo will enter negotiations to shelter under the French nuclear deterrent, becoming the first Nordic country to formally explore this option. The move reflects a structural break in European defence posture as confidence in the US security guarantee erodes across the continent.
WHY TODAY: Macron and Norwegian PM Stoere made the joint announcement Wednesday, formalizing what had previously been informal European conversations about non-US deterrence.

ECB vice-president warns Trump trade policies risk triggering a global financial crisis
ECB Vice-President Luis de Guindos said Washington's volatile trade policies and retreat from multilateral cooperation now constitute a systemic threat to financial stability, with crisis risk materially elevated. The warning from a major central bank is notable for its directness, signaling European policymakers now treat US policy as the primary tail risk rather than a manageable headwind.
WHY TODAY: De Guindos escalated ECB language on Wednesday from concern to an explicit financial crisis warning, a threshold the institution had not previously crossed.

06. CYBERSECURITY ●●●○○
FBI warns Silent Ransom Group is conducting in-person raids on US law firms to steal data
The FBI issued a public advisory Tuesday warning that the Silent Ransom Group is physically visiting US law firm offices, using social engineering to gain entry and exfiltrate sensitive client data. The pivot from remote compromise to in-person physical presence bypasses most technical controls and raises exposure for firms holding privileged legal and M&A information.
WHY TODAY: Tuesday's FBI advisory is the first public warning about this group's shift to physical-presence data theft, a novel escalation beyond conventional cyberattack vectors.

07. CYBERSECURITY ●●●○○
Iranian intelligence service identified as operator behind Los Angeles transit system breach
Researchers at Gambit Security attributed the hack of LA's transit network to Iran's Ministry of Intelligence, revealing the group had falsely presented itself as an independent hacktivist crew. State attribution converts the incident from opportunistic intrusion to deliberate foreign intelligence collection against US public infrastructure.
WHY TODAY: Gambit Security published its attribution report Tuesday, providing the first formal state-actor linkage to this breach and reframing its strategic significance.
SOURCES: The Record  •  READ →
08. CYBERSECURITY ●●●○○
CrowdStrike and Google disrupt Glassworm botnet built to survive conventional takedowns
Researchers dismantled the Glassworm botnet's command-and-control infrastructure, which used Solana blockchain transactions and BitTorrent DHT to resist law enforcement action while targeting open source software projects with malware. Disrupting the botnet removes a supply-chain attack vector that threatened the developers and organizations downstream of compromised open source packages.
WHY TODAY: CrowdStrike and Google jointly announced the takedown Wednesday, confirming the first successful disruption of a blockchain-anchored C2 infrastructure of this design.
SOURCES: BleepingComputer, TechCrunch  •  READ →

South Korea moves to commercialize Arctic shipping route by 2030 amid Hormuz disruption
South Korea announced plans to open a regular commercial Arctic route to Europe by 2030, following a trial voyage, as sustained Hormuz disruption forces a structural rethink of Asian supply chains. The convergence of Chinese and South Korean interest in the Northern Sea Route signals a durable shift in regional maritime strategy away from Middle East-dependent corridors.
WHY TODAY: South Korea's formal 2030 Arctic route target was announced Wednesday and explicitly framed as a response to ongoing Hormuz closure, moving the discussion from contingency planning to stated national policy.

10. SIGNAL ●●●●○
WHO warns Ebola outbreak in DR Congo faces catastrophic collision with active armed conflict
WHO Director-General Tedros warned that fighting in eastern DR Congo is severely hampering containment of an active Ebola outbreak, creating conditions for exponential spread into a displaced, under-resourced population. The combination of ongoing conflict, broken health infrastructure, and a live hemorrhagic fever outbreak raises genuine risk of a regional epidemic crossing borders.
WHY TODAY: Tedros issued the explicit 'catastrophic collision' warning Wednesday, escalating WHO public alarm to a threshold not previously used for this outbreak.
