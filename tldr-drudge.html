Romania confirmed a Russian drone, likely deflected by Ukrainian air defenses, struck a residential block on its territory. The incident marks a serious escalation, with debris landing inside a NATO member state and triggering formal condemnations from Brussels and the Alliance.

03. APAC ●●●●○
EU Launches Formal Tougher China Trade Approach as Beijing Threatens Retaliation
The European Commission agreed Friday on a new defensive trade posture toward China, with trade chief Maros Sefcovic presenting the case for stronger measures against what Brussels describes as unfair competition. Beijing immediately vowed retaliation, opening a potential new front in global trade tensions at a moment of deep supply-chain realignment.
WHY TODAY: The EC held a rare dedicated China trade debate Friday and formally agreed the new stance, the first such unified leadership-level decision.

04. CYBERSECURITY ●●●●○
Researcher Publishes Microsoft Zero-Day Exploits After Criminal Investigation Threat
A security researcher released multiple Microsoft zero-day vulnerabilities with working proof-of-concept code on GitHub after Microsoft reportedly threatened a criminal investigation, making the exploits immediately available to attackers. Microsoft publicly declared such disclosures are 'never justifiable,' intensifying a long-running dispute over vendor accountability and responsible disclosure norms.
WHY TODAY: The researcher escalated Friday by publishing live exploit code and threatening additional releases, forcing Microsoft into a public confrontation.

US Stocks Post Longest Weekly Winning Streak Since 2023 on AI and Iran Optimism
US equities closed their longest consecutive weekly winning run since 2023, driven by AI sector momentum and improving sentiment around a potential US-Iran ceasefire extension. The rally signals a broad recovery in risk appetite after months of tariff-driven volatility.
WHY TODAY: The fifth consecutive weekly gain was confirmed at Friday's close, the milestone not reached since late 2023.

06. MARKETS & MACRO ●●●○○
Bank of England Governor Warns Iran Ceasefire Would Still Leave Rate Path Uncertain
BoE Governor Andrew Bailey said Friday that even a successful US-Iran ceasefire would 'still create uncertainty,' signaling that interest rate cuts require significantly greater confidence before the Bank can act. The comments temper market expectations for near-term UK monetary easing and reflect broader central bank caution about geopolitically driven inflation.
WHY TODAY: Bailey made the remarks Friday in direct response to the Iran framework announced the same day, clarifying the BoE's reaction function in real time.

07. APAC ●●●○○
Former California Mayor Pleads Guilty to Acting as Unregistered Agent for China
Eileen Wang, who stepped down as mayor of Arcadia, California earlier this month, pleaded guilty Friday to acting as an unregistered agent of the Chinese government. The case is a rare successful prosecution of an elected US official for Chinese influence operations and underscores persistent PRC efforts to cultivate diaspora leverage.
WHY TODAY: The guilty plea was entered in federal court Friday, concluding the criminal phase of a case that began with charges in April.

Dutch Authorities Dismantle Botnet of 17 Million Devices and Seize Over 200 Servers
Dutch law enforcement took offline a botnet comprising 17 million infected devices and seized more than 200 servers from a local provider that supported the operation. The action is one of the largest single botnet disruptions in European history and removes a significant attack-for-hire infrastructure.
WHY TODAY: Dutch authorities announced the completed operation Friday.
