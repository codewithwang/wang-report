ENTERTAINMENT Joy Lee Senior Correspondent Wednesday, May 13, 2026

ABD is HYBE's replacement revenue vehicle, a label entity whose structure sits outside the ADOR litigation and whose trainee contracts do not inherit the contractual obligations that a US federal plagiarism complaint naming HYBE and NewJeans will, after this week's filing, put into discovery. US federal discovery runs broader than the Korean arbitration proceedings that had kept HYBE's IP licensing and branding contracts out of public record; the complaint achieves that exposure whether or not the underlying claim survives. NewJeans generated streaming, touring, and licensing revenue that HYBE's mid-tier label economics depended on; the group's trajectory after the 2024 ADOR management dispute removed that projection from the forecast. ABD is the replacement. The structural separation is the argument.

The capital timeline runs three to five years. HYBE's label economics require a debut act to generate streaming and licensing revenue at scale in its second year of release; if ABD's first group debuts in 2027, the label arrives at streaming-share negotiations with Spotify and Apple in 2028, which is also the period when the US litigation would be approaching verdict or settlement. YG's concurrent announcement of a boy group debut after a six-year gap confirms that the major Korean labels are treating 2026 as a replacement-cycle entry point; CJ ENM, absorbing streaming revenue pressure now, is building the same bet. The trainee pipeline costs that HYBE, YG, and CJ ENM are absorbing in 2026 will reach Spotify's K-pop licensing desk in 2028. That desk sets the rate.

Filing as written. The desk should hold this until the discovery timeline firms; the 2028 convergence is the story and it needs a date peg to run at full weight.-- WR
