Sixers landed Jaylen Brown. Read that sentence again and notice it does not include the word "trade," because as of Friday nobody has actually finished explaining what Boston received that makes this real money change hands rather than a fan-fiction headline sitting next to seven Mavericks transactions in the same news cycle. That is the tell. When Dallas is simultaneously flipping Santi Aldama, dangling AJ Johnson, chasing Marcus Sasser, and prepping a bid for Tyler Biberovic, you are not watching a front office build a roster. You are watching Nico Harrison run a going-out-of-business sale disguised as a rebuild, four months after trading Luka Doncic to the Lakers, in a league where Doncic's former employer still holds Dallas's incentive to keep him irrelevant on the schedule for exactly one more season under the Val Ancellotti trade restriction.

The number that actually matters here is the one nobody's repeating: Doncic to LA turned a supermax extension the Mavericks were on the hook for into a Lakers cap problem, and every subsequent Dallas move, Aldama out, Sasser in, Biberovic pursued, is a franchise quietly re-pricing itself around a superstar it no longer employs. Brown to Philadelphia, if it holds past the league's Sunday vetting window, does the opposite: it's Daryl Morey's front office betting max-money term relief buys two more years of contention around Embiid before the physical toll of that trade-off comes due. One team is unwinding a bet on a genius. The other just doubled down on one. Book the Lakers' opener against Dallas and watch which bet ages worse first.

Strong. The Val Ancellotti restriction detail and the LA cap-problem framing do the work most trade coverage skips.-- WR
