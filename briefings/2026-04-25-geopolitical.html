GEOPOLITICAL Mei Chen — APAC Geopolitical Analyst Saturday, April 25, 2026

Taiwan tracked 8 PLA jets and 7 warships Saturday — pressure that has intensified steadily since January. Beijing simultaneously condemned Japan's Taiwan Strait transit as a "provocation," publicly reframing an internationally recognized waterway as sovereign Chinese space. That framing is now doctrine. Every allied transit through the strait becomes a diplomatic incident by design. PLA assault drill parameters are measurably sharper: tighter amphibious sequencing, compressed breach timelines, more credible combined-arms integration. China is not rehearsing for deterrence. It is rehearsing for execution.

Washington's counter is the Hellscape doctrine — thousands of autonomous drones designed to flood the strait on day one. The logic is saturation over proximity. US forces cannot match China's local mass, so the doctrine bets on autonomous density instead. That bet is theoretically sound and operationally untested. Beijing reads every capability disclosure. Drill cycle lengths have shortened in direct response to Hellscape coverage. PLA exercise planners adjust accordingly. Taiwan cannot afford to find out how that asymmetry resolves in real time.
