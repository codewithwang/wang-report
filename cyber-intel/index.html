How to read these cards. Each vendor is a collectible card — Marvel card on the front for quick scanning, Magic card on the back for deep detail. Click any card to flip it.

Front (Marvel): The top shows the vendor monogram and security grade (A-F). Five horizontal bars rate patch velocity, disclosure quality, resilience, CVE posture, and concentration risk. The facts strip shows raw numbers. WR Featured vendors (gold border, holographic shimmer) include a one-sentence practitioner take — the editorial moat.

Back (Magic): MTG-inspired detail card. Mana cost encodes deployment complexity. The text box shows key stats as "abilities." The RRR strip — React, Respond, Resilient — rates incident behavior, not just vulnerability counts. This is what separates a CVE tracker from a risk publication.

Scoring: Grade is computed from Mean Time to Patch (30%), CISA KEV count (25%), zero-days (20%), critical CVE ratio (15%), and trend (10%). A ≥ 85, B ≥ 70, C ≥ 55, D ≥ 40, F < 40. Data sourced from NVD and CISA KEV feeds, updated daily via automated pipeline. Editorial ratings are manual — assigned by Harry Wang after real incidents.
