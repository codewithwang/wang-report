Scoring Methodology

How the Wang Report turns CVE telemetry into a single letter grade.

The Formula

Every vendor receives a composite score out of 100, computed from five weighted dimensions. The letter grade is derived from the composite.

DimensionWeightWhat it measures
Mean Time to Patch30%Days from CVE disclosure to vendor patch availability.
CISA KEV Exposure25%Number of the vendor's CVEs currently in CISA's Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog.
Zero-Day Activity20%CVEs exploited in the wild before a patch was available, within the 12-month window.
Critical CVE Ratio15%Share of the vendor's 12-month CVEs rated CRITICAL severity.
Year-over-Year Trend10%Whether the above metrics are improving, flat, or degrading versus the prior 12 months.

Sub-Score Bands

Mean Time to Patch

  • ≤ 7 days → 100 — Sector floor.
  • ≤ 14 days → 80 — Top quartile.
  • ≤ 30 days → 60 — Industry median.
  • ≤ 60 days → 35 — Remediation debt visible.
  • > 60 days → 10 — Structurally slow.

CISA KEV Exposure

  • 0 → 100 — Clean.
  • 1–2 → 70 — Isolated incidents.
  • 3–5 → 50 — Recurring presence.
  • 6–10 → 25 — Persistent catalog resident.
  • > 10 → 5 — Architectural.

Zero-Day Activity

  • 0 → 100 — No in-the-wild zero-days.
  • 1 → 75 — One observed.
  • 2–3 → 50 — Attacker interest durable.
  • 4+ → 20 — Known hunting ground.

Critical CVE Ratio

The percentage of the vendor's 12-month CVE list rated CRITICAL is inverted onto a 0–100 scale (0% critical → 100, 50%+ critical → 0).

Year-over-Year Trend

Direction of travel over four quarters. In V1 this defaults to a neutral value (50) pending a historical snapshot pipeline; future releases will compute this from the vendors.json history.

Letter Grade Thresholds

  • A — composite ≥ 85
  • B — composite ≥ 70
  • C — composite ≥ 55
  • D — composite ≥ 40
  • F — composite < 40

What is NOT in the Score

The composite is deliberately narrow. It does not include:

  • Company size, revenue, or market share.
  • Gartner, Forrester, or other analyst ratings.
  • Marketing materials, whitepapers, or vendor self-reporting.
  • Incident behaviour (React / Respond / Resilient). That is a separate, manually-assigned rating on top of the composite. It does not move the letter grade.

Data Sources

  • NVD — National Vulnerability Database, NIST. CVE identifiers, severity scoring, descriptions.
  • CISA KEV — Cybersecurity & Infrastructure Security Agency Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog.
  • Vendor advisories — Each vendor's published security bulletins for patch date confirmation.

Update Cadence

The scorecard rebuilds on every cron pass — twice daily at minimum. Vendor profile pages are regenerated in lockstep with the scorecard grid. Grades may move between runs as new CVEs land and patches ship.

Caveats

  • The 12-month window is rolling. A CVE that was "active" last month may drop off next month.
  • Sub-score bands are intentionally coarse. The goal is rank ordering, not fine-grained comparison between adjacent scores.
  • Vendors with small CVE counts are noisy. Treat grades on vendors with fewer than five 12-month CVEs as directional, not definitive.
  • Pack vendors (those with multiple products) take the worst-of module grade as the parent grade. See the per-profile module breakdown for variance.

Corrections

Data errors, missing CVEs, or vendor feedback: email corrections to the Wang Report editorial desk. This is a static publication, not a vendor review platform, so corrections flow through a single editorial queue rather than a comments system.
