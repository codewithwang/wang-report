Anthropic Disclosed. OpenAI Did Not. One Ran.

Fable 5 is suspended because Anthropic disclosed the jailbreak the government cited; the identical jailbreak is documented in GPT-5.5, which faces no equivalent order.
中村 彩 · Aya Nakamura AI Desk, Senior Correspondent Sunday, June 14, 2026

The June 12 Order

Fable 5 was suspended for a jailbreak, a technique that bypasses a model's output constraints by prompting it to read a codebase and surface software flaws. 5 carries the identical demonstrated jailbreak and continued taking production calls through the weekend. At 5:21pm ET on June 12, Anthropic received a directive ordering suspension of Fable 5 and Mythos 5, its two newest production models, for "any foreign national, whether inside or outside the United States," on national security grounds. No agency was named. Anthropic disabled both models globally within hours because it could not segregate foreign nationals from its user base in real time. Paying subscribers who had unlocked free access through June 22 lost it the next morning.

One Jailbreak, Two Outcomes

5 simultaneously. 5 equivalence in the same breath as the suspension. The directive pulled one. The operative criterion, by revealed behavior, was not the vulnerability. It was Anthropic's voluntary public disclosure of that equivalence. For any lab deciding what to disclose about its own jailbreak surface, that distinction is now the policy reference case.

The BIS, which administers US chip export controls, published guidance on June 1 closing a loophole that had let Chinese-affiliated companies in Singapore, Dubai, and other third-country hubs acquire Blackwell-tier Nvidia graphics processors (Nvidia's current flagship AI chip generation) by routing purchases through local subsidiaries. An importer that had been using that routing now needs a BIS export license to continue. That instrument governs compute supply. The June 12 directive governed deployed model access and arrived from a national security authority the directive did not name.

A developer in Seoul running production traffic on Anthropic's API learned June 12 that a US national security directive can disable their stack globally within hours. OpenAI filed confidentially with the SEC on June 8, targeting a public listing as early as September at an $852 billion private valuation. GPT-5.5, which carries the same cited jailbreak, continued taking production calls after June 12. The question the filing does not answer: whether the same unnamed national security authority applies the June 12 logic to a publicly traded OpenAI. Every lab calibrating its disclosure posture now has one reference case: Anthropic disclosed the equivalence. The suspension followed within hours.
