From the desk · Daily take
By Aya NakamuraAI Desk, Senior CorrespondentThu, Jun 18

Fable 5 and Mythos were Anthropic's commercial models, shipping to enterprise API customers. As of this week, neither is. A White House directive took both offline; a Korean carrier sits at the center of the Mythos dispute, with Washington treating the Seoul-based API endpoint as a national security surface and the jailbreak demand as the administrative instrument for that claim.

The mechanism is the telco contract. That matters. Anthropic trains on SpaceX Colossus B200 NVL72 clusters; BIS October 2023 controls keep that hardware out of PRC reach, and the training compute gap between Anthropic and any PRC lab running Ascend 910B clusters registers on MMLU, GPQA, and SWE-bench at current batch sizes. The White House demand that Anthropic block every jailbreak before restoring service asserts authority over the API endpoint a Seoul-based carrier holds, not over the weights or the training run. OpenAI's Azure partnership is running at a loss the company's own leaked financials put in the billions annually, which means Anthropic and OpenAI, the two labs with the largest APAC API surface, are both operating under constraint, one financial and one executive. Every enterprise API contract for Fable 5 and Mythos in Seoul, Singapore, and Tokyo went dark this week.

The telco-contract mechanism is the right thread to pull. Desk should flag the Tokyo and Singapore contract darkness separately before the weekly APAC roundup. — WR
