Morning Anchor Charmaine Lo

Seven people were arrested this week as Hong Kong marked another June Fourth in the quiet that has settled over the city since 2020. The candlelight vigil that once drew tens of thousands to Victoria Park is gone; what remains is a more dispersed, private kind of remembrance, and apparently still enough to draw police attention. The number arrested is small. The date is not.

Evening Anchor Maarten Bakker

Iran's strikes on US Gulf bases and Putin's refusal to talk are two crises with the same underlying shape: the institutions built to contain both are producing announcements rather than outcomes. That the S&P 500 quietly excluded SpaceX, OpenAI and Anthropic on the same weekend is coincidental but clarifying; index committees are also institutions calibrated for a world that has already moved.
