Morning Anchor Charmaine Lo

Hong Kong sent its first astronaut into orbit today, and the city slowed in the way it rarely allows itself to. The launch carries something that numbers do not capture: the quietly held belief, common among families across these districts, that a child from this city can reach anywhere. That pride is not complicated, and on a day like this, it does not need to be.

Evening Anchor Maarten Bakker

The Iran deal is largely done, then it is not, then Khamenei surrenders his uranium stockpile, then Tehran says no agreement is coming today. Meanwhile Russia fires a nuclear-capable missile at Kyiv, China parks a hundred warships off Taiwan, and Washington has apparently frozen arms deliveries to Taipei without telling anyone there. The structure of the day is not the individual headlines but the pattern: every major actor is simultaneously signalling resolution and escalation, because keeping both options open costs nothing yet.
