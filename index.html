Morning Anchor Charmaine Lo

Iran's closure of the Strait of Hormuz sent oil up seven percent overnight, and the figure that matters to Hong Kong is not the barrel price but what rerouting does to the shipping calendar. Vessels forced around the Arabian Peninsula add weeks to transit and cost to cargo, some of which originates or clears here. Energy shocks of this kind arrive quietly in this city, then all at once.

Three overlapping military campaigns and a collapsed negotiation in the same morning suggests the machinery of diplomacy and the machinery of war have been running on separate schedules for some time, and today the gap finally showed. Washington strikes Iranian radar sites while simultaneously losing the peace table; Tehran seals the Strait of Hormuz as an answer to both. Oil knows which track to believe.
