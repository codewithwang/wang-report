Morning Anchor Charmaine Lo

Hong Kong has overtaken Switzerland as the world's leading private wealth hub, and the figure lands differently when you live inside it. The city has always moved money, but this ranking makes visible what residents have sensed for some time: capital that once hedged elsewhere is settling here. Central feels it in the lobby conversations, the new addresses on business cards, the quiet expansion of family offices along the waterfront.

Evening Anchor Maarten Bakker

Three military theaters, two diplomatic ultimatums, and a Russian drone landing in Romania: the first day of June offers a useful reminder that deterrence is not a state but a performance, and that the audience has been growing visibly tired of the show. The gap between the language of red lines and the reality of incremental encroachment has become the defining institutional story of this decade, and today's dispatches do not suggest anyone has found a way to close it.
