Seventh Night of Strikes Hits a Kuwait Desalination Plant

The Iran war has moved from oil chokepoints to water infrastructure, and the American combat death toll now stands at sixteen with the fight still fought almost entirely from the air.

Read the synthesis →

Seven straight nights of US strikes on Iran, and the damage is spreading sideways rather than escalating upward. An Iranian strike hit a Kuwait desalination plant overnight, per AP, the first infrastructure hit that isn't oil or military. Gulf states run chronically water-short and depend on these plants for drinking supply, not just industry. Sixteen US service members are now dead, per Audacy, a toll that keeps climbing without a single ground engagement: this is an air and drone war on both sides. Iran's supreme leader promised Washington unforgettable lessons if strikes continue, while hardliners inside Tehran are reportedly accusing the leadership of a coup, per Hindustan Times. That fracture matters more than the rhetoric aimed outward. Hormuz war-risk insurance is the number this column has been waiting on for a week. It still hasn't moved. Underwriters have not repriced Gulf war-risk cover since the Kuwait hit on civilian water infrastructure, despite the shift in target set. A Gulf-exposed trade desk needs that pricing data before Monday's open, not the strike count. Watch whether insurers reprice by then.
