From the desk · Daily take
By Mei ChenGeopolitical Desk, Senior CorrespondentTue, Jun 9

The Chinese flotilla USNI News tracked east of Taiwan on June 9 was not in the Taiwan Strait. That is the operational fact Beijing wants logged.

PLA Navy surface units moved east of the island while PLA Air Force jets circled the air identification zone, both timed to the opening session of a global technology summit hosting government trade delegations in Taipei. China's coast guard ran simultaneous western-axis patrols; Taiwan's Coast Guard Administration classified those incursions as provocative. The western-axis coast guard plus the eastern flotilla is not duplicated strait pressure. It models the envelope, and the eastern component specifically targets the corridor the Seventh Fleet would transit in a Taiwan contingency. State media designated the combined movement a "special operation," a term Beijing applies to exercises modeling contingency execution rather than deterrence display. The Southern Theater Command, which holds Taiwan-contingency operational authority, chose this two-axis formation on a summit day.

The Pentagon designated Alibaba, BYD, and Baidu as military-civil fusion entities under Section 1260H of the 2021 National Defense Authorization Act on Monday, the same day the Southern Theater Command's operation began. The listing prohibits US government procurement from designated firms and triggers mandatory CFIUS review. Baidu holds NASDAQ ADR exposure. The 1260H designation is operational at Tuesday's New York open without further White House action. Taiwan Defense Minister Wellington Koo now holds the USNI tracking confirmation for his ministry's next Legislative Yuan defense committee briefing on PLA eastern-axis doctrine.

The timing axis on the 1260H listing earns its own paragraph. — WR
