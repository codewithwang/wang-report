From the desk · Daily take
MC
By Mei ChenGeopolitical Desk, Senior CorrespondentMon, Jun 1

Beijing's expansion of Taiwan combat patrols and concurrent Scarborough Shoal drills arrived within the same operational window as Japan-Philippines security talks, making Manila's bilateral calendar the variable Beijing is running against, not Taipei's procurement choices or Washington's theater posture. The PLA Eastern Theater Command issued both patrol escalations inside 48 hours of the Manila-Tokyo defense coordination announcement. Scarborough Shoal sits 240 kilometers from Subic Bay. The Japan-Philippines Reciprocal Access Agreement, signed January 2024, converts that proximity from a historical footnote into a live logistics question for any first-island-chain contingency. The PLA ran the numbers.

CENTCOM's strikes against Iranian air defenses, met this week by Tehran's missile response against 20 US military facilities, demonstrate the asset-allocation tradeoff Washington faces when two theaters demand forward posture at once. Beijing is watching this. Philippine Defense Secretary Gilberto Teodoro (in the position since August 2023) has staked his public posture on firmness in the West Philippine Sea, and his ministry's characterization of this week's Scarborough Shoal combat drills is the first observable test of whether that posture holds while Washington's bandwidth is committed elsewhere. Tokyo is the second variable. Japan's 2022 National Security Strategy budgeted 43 trillion yen over five years, and if Tokyo's Defense Ministry softens its characterization of PLA patrol escalation, Beijing registers it as confirmation that the Manila-Tokyo alignment is the seam the Scarborough drills were designed to probe.

The Manila-Tokyo alignment as the operative variable, not Taipei, is the correct read and most desks missed it. — WR
