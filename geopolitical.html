By Mei ChenGeopolitical Desk, Senior CorrespondentMon, Jun 1

Beijing's aerial refueler near Taiwan and its declared combat patrols at Scarborough Shoal are one operational picture, and Manila has read it correctly. The PLA tanker aircraft, tracked and reported Saturday by The National Interest, extends fighter patrol endurance beyond what a Taiwan Strait transit requires. Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense logged twelve Chinese warships in a 72-hour window ending Sunday. At Scarborough, Beijing's Coast Guard issued a "combat patrol" designation Friday, the first use of that language since the shoal entered Chinese administrative control in 2012. The Dutch frigate confrontation near the Spratly chain is the fourth data point. Beijing's Navy initiated it Thursday.

The Philippines' announced security pivot toward Taipei, reported by Bloomberg Sunday, is not a reorientation. It is a recognition. Beijing blocked the South China Sea Code of Conduct talks, the South China Morning Post reported Saturday, nine years into the formal negotiation cycle with no single-text draft signed. Manila concluded the diplomatic absorber is gone. Vietnam's accession to the BrahMos missile network, confirmed Friday, places a strike radius covering the southern approach to the Luzon Strait, and PLA Southern Theater Command now plans around a fires geometry that runs from the Ryukyus to the Bashi Channel without a gap.

The operational geometry holds across all four data points and the BrahMos addition closes the argument without overreach. — WR
