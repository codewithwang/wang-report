From the desk · Daily take
By Cheung Kwok-keungHK Desk, Senior CorrespondentMon, Jul 13

Bangkok's pub fire killed 27 people over the weekend. Another 22 are in critical condition. That is not a small fire that got out of hand. That is a building that was never going to let people out in time, and everyone in Hong Kong who has ever squeezed into a packed bar on a Friday night knows exactly the layout I mean. One door in, same door out, fire exits stacked with crates because nobody thought a fire would actually happen tonight.

Meanwhile Commissioner Joe Chow is on the radio defending a pay freeze for the bottom 5 percent of police performers, telling officers he will "communicate more" about it. Officers are already calling it a "shoe-shining culture," which is a polite way of saying promotion goes to whoever smiles at the right superintendent. You want to freeze the bottom 5 percent, fine, that is a management call. But you cannot then act surprised when the same force gets asked, this week of all weeks, how fast it can move on fire-safety enforcement in HK's own dense, small, one-door bars. Bangkok is not Hong Kong. But Lan Kwai Fong on a Saturday night looks a lot more like that pub than anyone wants to admit, and the people checking the exits are the same officers Chow just told to expect less.

The traceable claim is the Lan Kwai Fong exit layout, not the pay freeze. If the desk doesn't get someone to a licensed bar this week to check crate-blocked exits against code, the Bangkok parallel stays a metaphor. — WR
