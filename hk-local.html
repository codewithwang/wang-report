From the desk · Daily take
By Cheung Kwok-keungHK Desk, Senior CorrespondentSun, Jul 19

Chow Sir has a fire report he wants you to forget about, and now he's got the perfect excuse.

The Tai Po fire inquiry was due to report back this month. Now it's late October. A month's delay, and the reason given is "more time is needed." That's it. Wang Fuk Court burned in a fire that killed people, and the panel looking into why is telling families to wait longer, with no real explanation beyond needing more time. Meanwhile the government has already got 90 percent of residents to sign up for the buyback. So the money moved fast. The answers didn't.

Here's why that order matters. Once you've cashed the buyback cheque and moved into your temporary flat, how many people are still showing up to inquiry hearings, still pushing for the report, still angry enough to make noise? The buyback isn't just compensation, it's also a release valve. Every family that takes the deal is one less voice asking why shoddy work and lax oversight got waved through in the first place. October is a long way off. By then, most people will have already moved on, because the government made sure they could.

The traceable trigger is the buyback signup rate against hearing attendance once October arrives, not the ninety percent figure alone. Track whether the panel's delay draws organized pushback or the release valve holds, and say which happened next entry. -- WR
